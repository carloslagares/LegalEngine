# Chekin Compliance Hub -- Brand Voice Guidelines

## 1. Voice Dimensions

| Dimension             | Score (1-10) | Notes |
|-----------------------|:------------:|-------|
| Formal <-> Casual     | **3.5**      | Leans formal but stays approachable. Uses contractions ("We'll", "you'll", "it's") and second-person address, but avoids slang, humor, or colloquialisms. The tone is that of a knowledgeable professional speaking plainly. |
| Serious <-> Playful   | **2.5**      | Serious subject matter (fines, legal compliance, police reporting) treated with gravitas. No jokes, puns, or lighthearted copy. The seriousness is softened by clarity and empathy, not playfulness. |
| Technical <-> Simple  | **4**        | Balances domain-specific terms (Declaracion Responsable, vivienda de uso turistico, SES.HOSPEDAJES) with plain explanations. Never assumes the reader is a legal expert. Uses parenthetical clarifications ("tourism license (vivienda de uso turistico)"). |
| Reserved <-> Bold     | **6**        | Moderately bold. Uses urgency-driven statistics ("fines up to EUR 600,000", "30% of listings at risk") and direct imperatives ("Find your region", "Get registered now"). Headlines are clear but not aggressive. |

**Overall Voice Profile:** Professional Guide -- authoritative but never intimidating, direct but never pushy.

---

## 2. Brand Personality

**Primary Archetype: The Guide**

The Compliance Hub positions Chekin as a trusted guide who has already navigated the regulatory maze and is now leading the reader through it. Key signals:

- "We'll walk you through the registration process... step by step, no jargon, no guesswork."
- "Step-by-step instructions, document templates, and lawyer referrals."
- FAQ answers provide specific, factual detail rather than marketing spin.
- The AI chat interface acts as a personal advisor.

**Secondary Archetype: The Authority**

Credibility markers are woven throughout:
- "Trusted by 200,000+ properties worldwide"
- "Reviewed by licensed lawyers"
- "Updated for 2026 regulations"
- Specific fine amounts, legal references, and official platform names.

## 3. Tone by Context

### Hero Section
- **Tone:** Empathetic + confident. Acknowledges the user's position ("Your hosting journey starts with getting legal") and immediately promises clarity.
- **Pattern:** Benefit-first headline, followed by a reassurance subtitle that removes friction ("no jargon, no guesswork").
- **Observation:** The headline is warm but slightly generic. "Your hosting journey starts with getting legal" does not convey urgency or specificity compared to the rest of the page.

### Feature/Pain Point Cards
- **Tone:** Urgent + factual. Uses alarming statistics as hooks ("Fines up to EUR 600,000", "Listings removed without warning") then explains the consequence in plain terms.
- **Pattern:** Short, punchy headline (7 words or fewer) + 1-2 sentence explanation with a specific data point.
- **Strength:** This is the strongest copy on the page -- direct, evidence-based, and motivating.

### FAQ Answers
- **Tone:** Expert + helpful. Answers are thorough, cite specific legal mechanisms, and end with a soft CTA for Chekin where appropriate.
- **Pattern:** "Yes/No" opening, followed by context, then specific detail. The last FAQ answer ("Absolutely. Getting the license is just the first step...") smoothly transitions from education to product pitch.
- **Strength:** High-quality, trustworthy copy. Reads like advice from a knowledgeable colleague.

### CTA Buttons
- **Tone:** Action-oriented, low-pressure.
- **Primary CTAs:** "Find your region", "Try for free", "Book a demo"
- **Secondary CTAs:** "Talk to a lawyer", "Connect with a lawyer", "Or try Chekin free"
- **Observation:** CTAs are functional but lack power words. "Find your region" is directional but does not convey the value of what happens next. "Download guide" and "Show more articles" are purely descriptive.

### Blog/Resources Section
- **Tone:** Informational + SEO-driven. Titles follow the pattern "[Region]: [topic] guide [year]".
- **Observation:** The section title "Compliance blog" is generic and undersells the value. The description is functional but flat ("In-depth guides and articles on vacation rental regulations...").

### Chat Interface
- **Tone:** Conversational + supportive. The region picker prompt ("Which region are you interested in?") is clear and friendly. Error messages are reassuring ("Connection lost. Please try again.").
- **Observation:** The AI search input typewriter questions are well-crafted and demonstrate the tool's capabilities by example.

### Lawyer CTA Section
- **Tone:** Empathetic + premium. "Prefer to have an expert handle it?" validates the reader's choice without making them feel incapable.
- **Strength:** Trust indicators ("Free initial consultation", "Licensed in your region", "End-to-end registration") are well-placed and specific.

### Final CTA
- **Tone:** Straightforward wrap-up.
- **Observation:** "Ready to get started?" is among the most overused CTA headlines on the internet. It does not reinforce the page's unique value proposition.

### Words Notably Avoided
| Category | Examples NOT used |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Fear-mongering** | "Danger", "illegal", "arrested", "shut down" -- the page warns without terrorizing |

| **Casual/slang** | "Awesome", "super easy", "no brainer", "crush it" -- maintains professionalism |
| **Negative framing of the user** | Never implies the reader is doing something wrong; positions compliance as an opportunity |

## 5. Consistency Audit

### Strengths
- **Tone consistency:** The professional-guide voice is maintained across all sections. The FAQ, pain points, and feature descriptions all share the same factual, empathetic tone.
- **Second-person consistency:** Consistently addresses the reader as "you/your" throughout.
- **Data-driven approach:** Specific numbers appear everywhere (EUR 600,000 fines, 30% of listings, 200,000+ properties, 24 hours for guest reporting).

### Inconsistencies Found

1. **CTA energy mismatch:** The pain point section creates strong urgency (fines, listing removal), but the CTAs that follow are low-energy ("Find your region", "Ready to get started?"). The emotional arc drops at conversion points.

2. **Blog section voice drop:** The blog/resources section ("Compliance blog" with "In-depth guides and articles on...") shifts to a flat, catalog-like tone that contrasts with the engaging, empathetic voice elsewhere.

3. **Hero headline vs. rest of page:** "Your hosting journey starts with getting legal" is warmer and more aspirational than the rest of the page, which is more direct and urgent. The hero does not prepare the reader for the hard-hitting statistics that follow.

4. **"Show more articles" vs. other CTAs:** This button is purely functional while other CTAs have more polish. Minor but noticeable.

5. **Final CTA section is generic:** "Ready to get started?" + "Pick your region to download the free guide, or try Chekin to automate everything that comes after registration." This could appear on any SaaS landing page. It does not reinforce the compliance/legal expertise positioning.

## 7. Voice Guidelines for Future Copy

### Do
- Address the reader directly ("you", "your property", "your region")
- Lead with specific, verifiable facts (fine amounts, percentages, timelines)
- Explain legal terms in parentheses on first use
- Frame compliance as empowering, not burdensome ("build a business that lasts")
- Keep headlines under 10 words
- Use active voice and imperative mood in CTAs
- Pair urgency with a clear next step

### Don't
- Use fear-based language that makes the reader feel like a criminal
- Use marketing superlatives ("best", "ultimate", "revolutionary")
- Assume legal knowledge -- always provide context
- Use casual language, slang, or humor about legal consequences
- Make promises about outcomes Chekin cannot guarantee
- Write CTAs that are purely functional ("Submit", "Click here")

### Sentence Structure
- **Headlines:** Short, declarative, benefit-oriented. 5-10 words.
- **Subheads/descriptions:** 1-2 sentences. First sentence states the point; second adds evidence or context.
- **FAQ answers:** Open with a direct yes/no, explain why, give specifics, close with a Chekin tie-in where natural.
- **CTAs:** Verb + benefit. "Find your region" (good), "Download guide" (adequate), "Show more articles" (weak -- should convey benefit).

### Brand Voice in One Line
> **Authoritative but approachable. Fact-driven but human. Urgent but never alarmist.**
