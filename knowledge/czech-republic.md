# Czech Republic

VACATION RENTAL COMPLIANCE GUIDE -- CZECH REPUBLIC (CESKA REPUBLIKA)

This comprehensive guide covers all legal requirements for operating a short-term
vacation rental (kratkodoby pronajem) in the Czech Republic. It is written in a
step-by-step format to help property owners and managers achieve full compliance.

> Note: Regulations may change. Always verify current requirements with the
> relevant Czech authorities (Ministry of Industry and Trade, local municipality,
> Czech Tax Administration). Last reviewed: March 2026.

---

## Step 0: Understand the Legal Framework

Before starting any registration process, understand how Czech law classifies
short-term rental activity.

### Key Legislation

- **Trade Licensing Act (Zakon c. 455/1991 Sb., zivnostensky zakon)**: Governs
  trade licenses (zivnostensky list) required for accommodation services.
- **Act on Local Fees (Zakon c. 565/1990 Sb., o mistnich poplatcich)**: Governs
  the accommodation fee (poplatek z pobytu), amended significantly in 2020.
- **Act on the Residence of Foreign Nationals (Zakon c. 326/1999 Sb.)**: Mandates
  reporting of foreign guests to the Foreign Police via the UbyPort system.
- **Income Tax Act (Zakon c. 586/1992 Sb.)**: Governs taxation of rental income.
- **VAT Act (Zakon c. 235/2004 Sb.)**: Determines VAT obligations and thresholds.
- **Civil Code (Zakon c. 89/2012 Sb.)**: Provisions on lease agreements, property
  rights, and co-ownership.
- **Building Act (Zakon c. 183/2006 Sb., later Zakon c. 283/2021 Sb.)**: Governs
  building use classifications, zoning, and change-of-use permits.
- **Fire Protection Act (Zakon c. 133/1985 Sb.)**: Fire safety requirements for
  accommodation facilities.

### Classification of Activity

Czech law distinguishes between:

1. **Casual/occasional rental**: Renting out a property occasionally and
   infrequently (e.g., a few weeks per year) may be treated as income from
   rental under Section 9 of the Income Tax Act. No trade license required.
2. **Systematic accommodation services (ubytovaci sluzby)**: Regular,
   ongoing short-term rental activity is classified as a trade (zivnost) and
   requires a trade license. This applies to most Airbnb/Booking.com hosts
   who rent regularly.

The distinction is based on the **regularity, organization, and profit motive**
of the activity. The Czech Tax Administration and courts have generally held
that regular hosting on platforms like Airbnb constitutes a trade activity.

## Step 1: Obtain a Trade License (Zivnostensky List)

If you intend to operate short-term rentals regularly (which most vacation rental
hosts do), you must obtain a trade license.

### Type of Trade

Accommodation services (ubytovaci sluzby) fall under the category of
**"unregulated trades" (volna zivnost)** -- specifically, they are listed under
the free trade (volna zivnost) category in Annex 4 of the Trade Licensing Act.
The relevant activity group is typically:

- **"Provision of accommodation services" (Poskytovani ubytovacich sluzeb)**
  -- listed under activity field No. 55 in the free trade license.

This means no special qualifications or certifications are needed -- you simply
register.

### How to Obtain the License

1. **Visit the Trade Licensing Office (Zivnostensky urad)**: Go to the office
   in your municipality or at any Czech POINT (CzechPOINT -- universal
   administrative contact point).
2. **Submit the registration form (Jednotny registracni formular -- JRF)**:
   The unified registration form can also serve as your tax registration,
   social security registration, and health insurance registration.
3. **Required documents**:
   - Valid ID (passport or Czech ID card).
   - Proof of legal title to the premises (ownership deed, lease agreement
     with landlord's consent to subletting, etc.).
   - For foreigners: proof of residency permit (if applicable).
   - Clean criminal record (extract from the Criminal Register, or
     equivalent from your home country for foreigners).
4. **Fee**: CZK 1,000 (approximately EUR 40) for the initial trade license
   registration.
5. **Processing time**: Typically 5 working days. The trade license is valid
   from the date of registration (you can start operating immediately upon
   filing if all documents are in order).

### Registration Number (ICO)

Upon registering, you will receive an **ICO (Identifikacni cislo osoby)** --
a unique business identification number. This number is used for all official
interactions (tax filings, guest registration, platform reporting, etc.).

## Step 2: Register with the Tax Authority (Financni urad)

### Income Tax Registration

- If you used the JRF (unified registration form) at the Trade Licensing
  Office, your tax registration may be processed simultaneously.
- Otherwise, you must register with the local Tax Office (Financni urad)
  within 15 days of commencing your trade activity.
- You will receive a **DIC (Danove identifikacni cislo)** -- your tax
  identification number.

### VAT Registration

- **Mandatory VAT registration threshold**: If your annual turnover exceeds
  **CZK 2,000,000** (approximately EUR 80,000), you must register for VAT.
  Note: As of January 2025, the threshold was raised from CZK 1,000,000 to
  CZK 2,000,000 to align with EU harmonization efforts. Verify the current
  threshold with the Tax Office.
- **Voluntary VAT registration**: You may register voluntarily even below
  the threshold if it is advantageous (e.g., to reclaim input VAT on
  renovations).
- **VAT rate on accommodation services**: The reduced VAT rate of **12%**
  applies to accommodation services (ubytovaci sluzby). This was consolidated
  from the previous two reduced rates (10% and 15%) into a single 12% rate
  effective January 2024.

### Income Tax Rates

- **Individuals (DPFO)**: Progressive rates of **15%** on income up to
  CZK 1,935,552 (36x the average wage) and **23%** on income above that
  threshold (as of 2024/2025).
- **Corporations (DPPO)**: Flat rate of **21%** (raised from 19% effective
  January 2024).
- **Flat-rate expense deduction (pausalni vydaje)**: If you do not keep full
  accounting records, you may deduct a flat percentage of gross revenue as
  expenses. For accommodation services (trade income under Section 7 of the
  Income Tax Act), the flat-rate deduction is **60%** of gross revenue, up
  to a maximum of CZK 1,200,000.
- **Social and health insurance contributions**: As a self-employed person
  (OSVC), you must pay social security and health insurance contributions.
  Minimum advance payments apply even in the first year of business.

## Step 3: Register with the Local Municipality (Obec)

### Accommodation Fee Registration (Poplatek z pobytu)

You must register with the local municipality as an accommodation fee
collector. This is mandatory in municipalities that have enacted the local
accommodation fee.

1. **Notify the municipality**: Register as an "accommodation provider"
   (poskytovateli ubytovani) with the municipal authority (obecni urad /
   magistrat) within 30 days of commencing your rental activity.
2. **Keep a guest book (domovni kniha / kniha hostu)**: You must maintain a
   record of all guests including:
   - Full name
   - Date of birth
   - Address of permanent residence
   - ID / passport number
   - Check-in and check-out dates
   - Purpose of stay
3. **Collect and remit the accommodation fee**: You collect the fee from
   guests and remit it to the municipality on a periodic basis (monthly,
   quarterly, or annually -- as determined by the municipal ordinance).

### Accommodation Fee Rates (Poplatek z pobytu)

Since January 1, 2020, the amended Act on Local Fees allows municipalities to
set the accommodation fee at up to **CZK 50 per person per night** (previously
the combined spa/accommodation fee cap was lower). Each municipality decides
whether to levy the fee and at what rate.

**Key city rates (verify with each municipality as rates may change annually):**

| City | Rate per person/night | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| Prague (Praha) | CZK 50 | Maximum rate applied since 2022 |
| Brno | CZK 50 | Maximum rate |
| Cesky Krumlov | CZK 50 | Maximum rate; high tourism area |
| Karlovy Vary | CZK 50 | Spa town; maximum rate |
| Olomouc | CZK 30 | Verify current rate |
| Plzen | CZK 30 | Verify current rate |
| Ostrava | CZK 20-30 | Verify current rate |
| Liberec | CZK 21 | Verify current rate |

**Exemptions** (generally standard across municipalities):
- Persons under 18 years of age
- Persons hospitalized in medical facilities
- Persons with disabilities (holders of ZTP/P cards)
- Persons accommodated in facilities for social services
- Persons whose stay is ordered by court or authority
- Specific municipal exemptions may vary

## Step 4: Police Registration -- UbyPort System

### Mandatory Reporting of Foreign Guests

Under the Act on the Residence of Foreign Nationals, every accommodation
provider must report foreign guests to the Foreign Police (Cizinecka policie).

### UbyPort System

**UbyPort** is the official electronic system for registering accommodated
foreigners, operated by the Czech Foreign Police.

1. **Register in UbyPort**: Before accommodating any foreign guests, you must
   register your accommodation facility in the UbyPort system at
   **https://ubyport.policie.cz**.
2. **Required information for registration**:
   - Trade license details (ICO, zivnostensky list number)
   - Address of the accommodation facility
   - Contact details of the operator
   - Capacity of the facility (number of beds/rooms)
3. **Reporting obligations**:
   - Report each foreign guest **within 3 working days** of their arrival
     (some sources indicate this must be done within 24 hours for certain
     nationalities -- verify current rules with the Foreign Police).
   - Information to report: guest's full name, date of birth, citizenship,
     travel document number, visa/residence permit details, date of arrival,
     expected date of departure.
4. **Reporting methods**:
   - **Online via UbyPort**: The preferred and most common method. You log
     into the system and enter guest data electronically.
   - **Paper form**: In exceptional cases, you may submit paper forms
     (Prihlasovaci listek) at the local Foreign Police office.
5. **Czech citizens**: While UbyPort specifically covers foreign nationals,
   you are still obligated to maintain a guest book (domovni kniha) that
   includes Czech citizens as well (for accommodation fee purposes and
   general record-keeping).

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

Failure to report foreign guests can result in fines of up to **CZK 50,000**
for individuals and higher amounts for legal entities.

## Step 5: Prague-Specific Regulations

Prague has been at the forefront of regulating short-term rentals in the Czech
Republic due to the significant impact on housing availability and neighborhood
livability.

### Current Regulatory Landscape

- **Prague Municipal Ordinance**: Prague applies the maximum accommodation fee
  of CZK 50 per person per night.
- **Zoning and building use**: Prague's municipal districts (mestske casti)
  may enforce zoning rules more strictly. Operating a short-term rental in a
  building zoned exclusively for residential use may require a change-of-use
  permit (zmena ucelu uzivani) -- see Step 9 on building regulations.
- **Community of unit owners (SVJ -- Spolecenstvi vlastniku jednotek)**:
  In apartment buildings, the SVJ (homeowners' association) may restrict or
  ban short-term rental activity through amendments to the building's statutes
  (stanovy). Czech courts have upheld the right of SVJs to impose such
  restrictions since a key Supreme Court ruling. Always check your building's
  statutes before commencing operations.

### Proposed / Recent Regulatory Changes

- **Draft legislation on platform regulation**: The Czech government and Prague
  city authorities have discussed various proposals to tighten regulation of
  platforms like Airbnb, including:
  - Mandatory registration numbers displayed on listings
  - Day-caps on short-term rentals (similar to Amsterdam or Paris models)
  - Enhanced data-sharing obligations for platforms
- **EU Short-Term Rental Regulation (EU 2024/1028)**: The EU regulation on
  short-term rental data collection, adopted in 2024, requires Member States
  to implement a registration system and platform data-sharing obligations by
  2026. The Czech Republic is expected to transpose this regulation, which will
  likely introduce:
  - A national or municipal registration system for short-term rental
    properties
  - Mandatory display of registration numbers on platform listings
  - Automated data sharing between platforms and competent authorities
  - Activity-related data reporting by platforms (number of nights, income)

### Prague District Enforcement

Some Prague districts (notably Praha 1 and Praha 2) have been more active in
enforcement, conducting inspections of suspected unlicensed accommodation
services and working with the Trade Licensing Office and Tax Authority to
identify non-compliant operators.

## Step 6: EET -- Electronic Registration of Sales (Elektronicka evidence trzeb)

### Current Status: ABOLISHED

The EET system, which required businesses to electronically report every cash
transaction to the Tax Administration in real time, was **abolished effective
January 1, 2023**. The law repealing EET (Zakon c. 458/2022 Sb.) was passed
in late 2022.

**Key points:**

- You are **no longer required** to use EET cash registers or report individual
  sales transactions electronically.
- The obligation to issue receipts remains under general accounting and tax
  rules, but the specific EET real-time reporting requirement is gone.
- You must still keep proper accounting records or tax evidence (danova
  evidence) for income tax purposes.

## Step 7: Tax Obligations -- Detailed Breakdown

### Income Classification

Your rental income is classified differently depending on your setup:

| Scenario | Tax Section | Flat-Rate Deduction |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| Trade license holder (regular STR) | Section 7 (trade income) | 60% of revenue |
| Occasional rental (no trade license) | Section 9 (rental income) | 30% of revenue |
| Corporate entity (s.r.o.) | Corporate income tax | Actual expenses only |

### Annual Tax Filing

- **Deadline**: April 1 of the following year (extended to May 1 if filed
  by a tax advisor, or July 1 if filed electronically via a tax advisor).
- **Advance payments**: If your tax liability exceeds CZK 30,000, you must
  make semi-annual advance payments. Above CZK 150,000, quarterly advance
  payments are required.

### Social Security and Health Insurance (OSVC)

As a self-employed person (OSVC):

- **Health insurance**: Minimum monthly advance of approximately CZK 2,968
  (2024 rate; adjusted annually). You must pay at least the minimum even if
  your income is low.
- **Social security (socialni pojisteni)**: Minimum monthly advance of
  approximately CZK 3,852 (2024 rate). Mandatory if your trade is your main
  activity. If accommodation is your side activity (vedlejsi cinnost -- e.g.,
  you have a full-time job), social security advances are required only if
  your annual profit exceeds a threshold (approximately CZK 105,520 for 2024).

### Road Tax / Other Taxes

If you use a vehicle for your rental business (e.g., guest transfers), you may
be subject to road tax (silnicni dan). Note that road tax was significantly
reformed -- since 2022, it applies primarily to heavier vehicles (over 3.5
tonnes), so most passenger cars are exempt.

## Step 8: Safety and Hygiene Requirements

### Fire Safety

- **Fire safety documentation**: Accommodation facilities must have fire safety
  documentation (pozarne bezpecnostni reseni) prepared by a qualified fire
  safety technician (pozarni technik).
- **Fire extinguishers**: At least one approved fire extinguisher per floor or
  rental unit.
- **Smoke detectors**: Recommended and increasingly required, especially in
  buildings undergoing reconstruction or change of use.
- **Emergency exit marking**: Clear marking of emergency exits and evacuation
  routes.
- **Fire escape plan**: Must be displayed in a visible location within the
  accommodation unit.
- **Fire revisions**: Regular inspection and maintenance of electrical
  installations, gas appliances, chimneys, and heating systems per applicable
  technical standards (revizni zpravy).

### Hygiene Standards

- **Regional Hygiene Station (Krajska hygienicka stanice -- KHS)**: The KHS
  may inspect accommodation facilities for compliance with hygiene standards.
  Requirements include:
  - Clean bed linen and towels provided to each guest (changed at minimum
    between guest stays, or weekly for longer stays).
  - Adequate ventilation in all rooms.
  - Functional bathroom and toilet facilities.
  - Drinking water supply meeting quality standards.
  - Waste management -- proper disposal of household waste.
- **Classification not mandatory**: Unlike hotels, vacation rentals
  (ubytovaci sluzby in private apartments) are not required to obtain a
  formal star classification. However, they must meet basic hygiene and
  habitability standards.

### Technical Standards for Accommodation

- Minimum room temperature capability of 20 degrees C during heating season.
- Adequate lighting (natural and artificial) in all habitable rooms.
- Hot and cold running water in the bathroom/kitchen.
- Functioning locks on entrance doors.

## Step 9: Building Regulations and Zoning

### Change of Use (Zmena ucelu uzivani)

- If a building or unit is officially designated for **residential use (bydleni)**
  in the building register (katastr nemovitosti) and building permit, operating
  it as an accommodation facility may require a **change-of-use notification
  (oznameni zmeny v uzivani stavby)** or a full change-of-use permit.
- The requirement depends on:
  - The **scope and intensity** of the rental activity.
  - Whether the building authority considers short-term rental to constitute
    "accommodation services" (which is a different use category than
    "housing").
  - The specific building authority's interpretation -- this varies across
    municipalities.
- **Process**: Submit a notification to the local Building Authority
  (stavebni urad). The authority may require:
  - Binding opinions from the Fire Safety Authority and KHS (hygiene).
  - Consent from neighboring property owners (in some cases).
  - Documentation that the premises meet technical requirements for
    accommodation use.

### Co-ownership and SVJ Restrictions

- In a **bytove spoluvlastnictvi** (unit co-ownership / condominium), the
  SVJ (homeowners' association) can adopt restrictions on short-term
  rentals through its statutes.
- A **three-quarters majority (75%)** of unit owners at the SVJ assembly
  is typically required to amend the statutes.
- Check the **prohlaseni vlastnika** (declaration of the owner / building
  declaration) and the SVJ statutes before proceeding.

### Landlord Consent

If you are a **tenant** (najemce) and wish to sublet for short-term rentals:
- You need **written consent from the landlord** to sublet (podnajemni
  smlouva or consent clause in the lease).
- Subletting without consent is grounds for lease termination.

## Step 10: Insurance Requirements

### Mandatory and Recommended Insurance

There is no single "mandatory vacation rental insurance" law in the Czech
Republic, but the following are essential:

1. **Property insurance (pojisteni nemovitosti)**: Standard homeowner
   insurance. Verify that your policy covers commercial use / accommodation
   services. Many standard residential policies exclude or limit coverage
   for business activities.
2. **Liability insurance (pojisteni odpovednosti za skodu)**: Covers damage
   or injury to guests or third parties. Highly recommended and may be
   required by your SVJ or building insurer.
3. **Contents insurance (pojisteni domacnosti)**: Covers furnishings,
   appliances, and personal property against damage by guests.
4. **Business interruption insurance**: Optional but recommended for
   properties generating significant revenue.

### Key Considerations

- Notify your insurer that you are using the property for short-term rental /
  accommodation services. Failure to disclose this may void your policy.
- Some platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com) provide limited host protection
  programs, but these should not replace dedicated insurance coverage.

## Step 11: Platform Reporting Obligations

### Current Obligations

- **DAC7 (EU Directive 2021/514)**: Since January 2023, digital platforms
  (Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo, etc.) are required to collect and report
  information about sellers/hosts to tax authorities across the EU. This
  includes:
  - Host identity (name, address, date of birth, tax ID)
  - Property address
  - Total consideration (revenue) paid to the host
  - Number of rental days
  - Bank account details
- This means the Czech Tax Administration (Financni sprava) automatically
  receives data about your rental activity from platforms. Undeclared income
  is easily detectable.

### EU Short-Term Rental Regulation (Regulation 2024/1028)

- Adopted in 2024, Member States must implement by **May 2026**:
  - Hosts must obtain a **registration number** from the competent authority.
  - Platforms must **display the registration number** on listings.
  - Platforms must **verify registration numbers** and remove listings
    without valid numbers.
  - Platforms must share **activity data** (nights rented, revenue, guest
    counts) with authorities.
  - Authorities can **order removal** of non-compliant listings.
- The Czech Republic is expected to establish the necessary registration
  framework and single digital entry point as required by the regulation.

### Display Requirements

- Once a registration system is in place, you will need to display your
  registration number on all platform listings and advertisements.
- Currently, some platforms already require you to provide your trade license
  number (ICO) or other identification when creating a listing.

## Step 12: Sanctions and Fines

### Trade License Violations

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Operating without a trade license | Up to CZK 500,000 (approx. EUR 20,000) |
| Failure to notify the Trade Licensing Office of changes | Up to CZK 100,000 |

### Tax Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Late tax filing | Penalty of 0.05% of tax due per day (min. CZK 500, max 5% of tax or CZK 300,000) |
| Undeclared income | Additional tax assessment + penalty of 20% of additionally assessed tax |
| Tax evasion (criminal) | Criminal prosecution possible for amounts exceeding CZK 100,000 |
| Late VAT registration | Retroactive VAT liability + penalties |

### Accommodation Fee Violations

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Failure to register as fee collector | Up to CZK 500,000 |
| Failure to collect or remit the fee | Up to CZK 500,000 |
| Failure to maintain guest book | Up to CZK 500,000 |

### UbyPort / Foreign Guest Reporting Violations

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Failure to report foreign guests | Up to CZK 50,000 (individuals) |
| Repeated / systematic non-reporting | Higher fines; potential license implications |

### Building and Zoning Violations

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Unauthorized change of use | Up to CZK 500,000 |
| Operating in violation of zoning | Orders to cease activity + fine |

### Hygiene Violations

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Non-compliance with hygiene standards (KHS) | Up to CZK 100,000 |

## Step 13: Recent Regulatory Changes and Outlook

### Changes Already in Effect

1. **EET abolished (January 2023)**: No more electronic sales registration
   requirement.
2. **VAT consolidation (January 2024)**: Two reduced VAT rates merged into
   a single 12% rate for accommodation services.
3. **Corporate tax increase (January 2024)**: Rate increased from 19% to 21%.
4. **VAT threshold increase (January 2025)**: Mandatory VAT registration
   threshold raised from CZK 1,000,000 to CZK 2,000,000 annual turnover.
5. **DAC7 platform reporting (January 2023)**: Platforms now report host
   income data to tax authorities across the EU.
6. **Accommodation fee reform (January 2020)**: Single "poplatek z pobytu"
   replaced the former dual system (spa fee + accommodation fee), with
   a maximum cap of CZK 50 per person per night.

### Upcoming / Expected Changes

1. **EU Short-Term Rental Regulation transposition (by May 2026)**: The
   Czech Republic must implement a registration system for short-term
   rental properties, platform data-sharing mechanisms, and listing
   verification procedures.
2. **Potential Prague-specific restrictions**: Prague city authorities
   continue to discuss additional measures such as day-caps, enhanced
   zoning enforcement, and mandatory registration at the municipal level.
3. **SVJ rights clarification**: Ongoing legal developments regarding
   the extent to which homeowners' associations can restrict short-term
   rentals through their statutes.

## Quick-Reference Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure you have completed all required steps:

- [ ] Determine if your activity qualifies as a trade (regular STR = yes)
- [ ] Obtain a trade license (zivnostensky list) at the Trade Licensing Office
- [ ] Receive your ICO (business identification number)
- [ ] Register with the Tax Office (Financni urad) and obtain your DIC
- [ ] Assess VAT obligations (threshold: CZK 2,000,000 annual turnover)
- [ ] Register with the local municipality as an accommodation fee collector
- [ ] Set up a guest book (domovni kniha) for all guests
- [ ] Register in the UbyPort system for foreign guest reporting
- [ ] Check building use designation -- obtain change-of-use if needed
- [ ] Check SVJ statutes (apartment buildings) for any rental restrictions
- [ ] Obtain landlord consent if you are a tenant
- [ ] Arrange appropriate insurance (property, liability, contents)
- [ ] Comply with fire safety requirements (extinguishers, escape plans)
- [ ] Meet hygiene standards (clean linens, ventilation, sanitation)
- [ ] Display your ICO / future registration number on platform listings
- [ ] File annual tax returns and pay social/health insurance contributions
- [ ] Collect and remit the accommodation fee (poplatek z pobytu) to the municipality
- [ ] Report foreign guests via UbyPort within the required timeframe
