# France

VACATION RENTAL COMPLIANCE GUIDE FOR FRANCE
This guide provides property owners and managers with a step-by-step overview of the
administrative, legal, and tax requirements for operating a short-term furnished rental
(meuble de tourisme) in France. It incorporates the significant reforms introduced by the
Loi Le Meur (loi n 2024-1039 du 19 novembre 2024), which took effect progressively from
late 2024 through 2025.

Disclaimer: This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal
advice. Regulations vary by commune and are subject to change. Consult a qualified
professional or your local mairie for guidance specific to your situation.

---

## Step 1: Understand the Legal Classification -- Meuble de Tourisme

A meuble de tourisme is a furnished dwelling unit (villa, apartment, studio) rented on a
short-term basis to transient guests who do not establish their primary residence there.
This is defined in Article L324-1 of the Code du tourisme.

Key distinctions:
- A meuble de tourisme is rented in its entirety (not individual rooms -- that falls
  under chambre d'hote rules).
- Rentals are short-term by nature, typically by the night, week, or month, to a
  clientele that does not take up permanent residence.
- It differs from a bail mobilite (furnished lease of 1-10 months) and a standard
  furnished rental (bail meuble of at least 1 year).

### Voluntary Star Classification (Classement)

Owners may apply for an optional star classification (1 to 5 stars) from an accredited
inspection body (organisme accredite par le COFRAC). The classification is valid for 5
years.

Benefits of classification:
- Higher tax allowance under the micro-BIC regime (see Step 9).
- Greater visibility on tourism platforms and local tourism office listings.
- Access to the "Meuble de Tourisme Classe" label.

Important change under Loi Le Meur: Starting from 2025, the tax advantage for classified
meubles de tourisme has been significantly reduced. The micro-BIC abatement for classified
properties dropped from 71% to 50% (with revenue capped at 77,700 EUR). For unclassified
properties, the abatement dropped from 50% to 30% (with revenue capped at 15,000 EUR).
These new rates apply to income earned from January 1, 2025 onward.

## Step 2: Declaration Prealable at the Mairie (Town Hall Registration)

### Who Must Declare

Since the Loi Le Meur reforms, ALL owners renting a meuble de tourisme must file a
declaration with their local mairie, regardless of whether the property is a primary
residence or a secondary residence. Previously, only properties in certain communes were
subject to this requirement, but the law has made it universal across all of France.

### How to Declare

The declaration is made using a CERFA form (formerly CERFA n 14004, now being updated
to reflect Loi Le Meur changes). It is submitted to the mairie of the commune where the
property is located.

Information required:
- Identity of the owner (or the tenant subletting with owner authorization).
- Address and description of the property.
- Number of rooms and maximum guest capacity.
- Whether the property is the declarant's primary residence (residence principale) or not.
- Planned rental periods.

### Numero d'Enregistrement (Registration Number)

Upon filing the declaration, the mairie issues a numero d'enregistrement (registration
number). This number is a 13-character identifier.

Obligations tied to the registration number:
- The registration number MUST be displayed on every listing and advertisement for
  the property, whether on platforms like Airbnb, Booking.com, Abritel, or on the
  owner's own website.
- Platforms are legally required to verify that listings display a valid registration
  number and to block listings that do not.
- The registration number is valid for a limited duration and must be renewed. Under
  the Loi Le Meur, the mairie may suspend or revoke the registration number if the
  owner is found to be in violation of local rules (e.g., exceeding the 120-day limit).

### Loi Le Meur Change -- National Registration System

The Loi Le Meur mandates the creation of a unified national online registration system
(teleservice national) managed by a single digital platform. This system is expected to
be fully operational by 2026. It will standardize the declaration and registration number
process across all communes, replacing the patchwork of local systems.

## Step 3: Changement d'Usage Rules (Change of Use Authorization)

### When It Applies

In communes with more than 200,000 inhabitants (Paris, Lyon, Marseille), and in communes
located in "zones tendues" (high-demand housing zones) where the local council has decided
to apply these rules, renting a property that is NOT the owner's primary residence as a
short-term rental requires a prior authorization for changement d'usage (change of use)
from residential to commercial.

This applies to cities including but not limited to:
- Paris
- Lyon
- Marseille
- Bordeaux
- Nice
- Toulouse
- Nantes
- Montpellier
- Strasbourg
- Lille

Under the Loi Le Meur, any commune -- not just those with more than 200,000 inhabitants --
may now decide to implement changement d'usage requirements by passing a local council
deliberation. This significantly expands the potential geographic scope.

### The Compensation Mechanism

In Paris and several other large cities, the changement d'usage authorization comes with
a compensation requirement: the owner must convert an equivalent (or greater) surface area
of commercial space into residential use to "compensate" for the loss of housing stock.

In Paris specifically:
- The compensation ratio in most arrondissements is 1:1 (1 square meter of commercial
  space converted to residential for every 1 square meter of residential converted to
  short-term rental).
- In "reinforced compensation" sectors (arrondissements 1-4 and certain parts of other
  central arrondissements), the ratio can be 2:1 or even 3:1.
- Compensation can be achieved by purchasing "commercialite" (commercial use rights)
  from another property owner who is converting commercial space to residential.
- The cost of commercialite in Paris can range from 1,000 to 3,000+ EUR per square
  meter, making it extremely expensive.

### Primary Residence Exemption

If the property is the owner's primary residence (the place where the owner lives at
least 8 months per year), the changement d'usage authorization is NOT required. However,
the 120-day annual limit applies (see Step 4).

### Loi Le Meur Change -- Quotas and Zoning

The Loi Le Meur gives communes expanded powers:
- Communes may establish geographic quotas limiting the number or proportion of meubles
  de tourisme in certain neighborhoods or zones.
- Communes may designate zones where new meubles de tourisme authorizations are
  suspended entirely.
- Communes may reduce the maximum rental period for primary residences below the
  national default of 120 days (with a floor -- communes cannot set it below 90 days).
- Coproprietes (homeowner associations / condominium associations) may now more easily
  vote to prohibit short-term rental activity in their buildings. A two-thirds majority
  vote is sufficient to amend the reglement de copropriete to ban meubles de tourisme.

## Step 4: The 120-Day Annual Limit for Primary Residences

### The Rule

When a property that is the owner's primary residence is rented as a meuble de tourisme,
it may not be rented for more than 120 days per calendar year. This rule is codified in
Article L324-1-1 of the Code du tourisme.

Primary residence is defined as the dwelling occupied at least 8 months per year by the
owner (or tenant, with landlord authorization), except for professional obligations,
health reasons, or force majeure.

### Enforcement

- Platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.) are legally obligated to block bookings that
  would cause a listing to exceed 120 days in a calendar year. They must transmit
  rental day counts to the commune upon request.
- The mairie can request booking data from platforms to verify compliance.
- If the property is NOT a primary residence, there is no 120-day limit, BUT the
  property requires changement d'usage authorization where applicable (see Step 3).

### Loi Le Meur Change

Communes now have the power to reduce this limit below 120 days, down to a minimum of
90 days, by local council decision. Some popular tourist areas may choose to exercise
this option. Additionally, communes gain the right to require proof that the property is
genuinely the host's primary residence (e.g., tax notices, utility bills).

## Step 5: Taxe de Sejour (Tourist Tax)

### Obligation

All meuble de tourisme operators must collect the taxe de sejour (tourist tax) from
guests and remit it to the commune. This tax is per person, per night, and varies by
commune and by property classification.

### Rates (2025 Applicable Rates)

Rates are set by each commune within ranges established by national law. Typical ranges:

| Classification                | Rate Range (per person/night) |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 5-star                        | 0.50 - 4.00 EUR              |
| 4-star                        | 0.50 - 2.53 EUR              |
| 3-star                        | 0.50 - 1.50 EUR              |
| 2-star                        | 0.30 - 0.90 EUR              |
| 1-star                        | 0.20 - 0.75 EUR              |
| Unclassified meuble tourisme  | 1% - 5% of nightly rate      |

For unclassified meubles de tourisme, the taxe de sejour is typically calculated as a
percentage of the nightly rate (excluding charges), per person per night, capped at the
highest rate applicable to 5-star properties in that commune.

Exemptions: Children under 18 are generally exempt.

### Collection and Remittance

- If you list on major platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, Abritel/HomeAway), the platform
  typically collects and remits the taxe de sejour on your behalf automatically. Verify
  this with the specific platform.
- If you rent directly (own website, word of mouth), YOU are responsible for collecting
  the tax from guests and remitting it to the commune, typically on a quarterly,
  semi-annual, or annual basis depending on the commune.
- You must maintain a register (registre du logeur) recording each guest stay: dates,
  number of guests, amounts collected.

### Loi Le Meur Change

The Loi Le Meur allows communes to increase the taxe de sejour for unclassified meubles
de tourisme up to a cap of 5% of the nightly price per person. Some communes may raise
rates to discourage short-term rentals in high-demand areas.

## Step 6: Police Registration of Foreign Guests (Fiche Individuelle de Police)

### Obligation

Under Article R611-42 of the Code de l'entree et du sejour des etrangers et du droit
d'asile (CESEDA), all operators of meubles de tourisme must require foreign guests
(non-French nationals) to complete a fiche individuelle de police (individual police
form) upon arrival.

### Required Information

The fiche must include:
- Full name (nom and prenom).
- Date and place of birth.
- Nationality.
- Usual place of residence (home address).
- Passport or identity document number, date and place of issue.
- Date of arrival.
- Planned date of departure.
- For guests accompanied by children under 15: the children's full names.

### Retention and Transmission

- The fiches must be kept for a period of 6 months.
- They must be presented to police or gendarmerie upon request.
- Some communes require periodic transmission of fiches to the local commissariat or
  gendarmerie. Check with your local mairie.

### Practical Note

French nationals and EU citizens with a valid national ID card are often subject to
lighter identification checks in practice, but the legal obligation technically extends
to all foreign guests (i.e., anyone not of French nationality).

## Step 7: DPE -- Diagnostic de Performance Energetique (Energy Performance Diagnostic)

### Current Requirement

A valid DPE (energy performance diagnostic) is required for all rental properties in
France, including meubles de tourisme. The DPE rates the energy performance of the
property on a scale from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient) and must be performed
by a certified diagnostician.

The DPE must be:
- Included in the property listing or advertisement.
- Made available to guests.
- Valid (a DPE is valid for 10 years, but older DPEs made before July 1, 2021 under
  the previous methodology may have expired or been invalidated early).

### Loi Le Meur -- New DPE Minimums for Meubles de Tourisme

The Loi Le Meur introduces minimum energy performance thresholds for meubles de
tourisme, aligning short-term rentals with rules already in place for long-term rentals:

- From January 1, 2025: New meubles de tourisme (first-time registrations) must have
  a DPE rating of at least F or better (i.e., G-rated properties cannot be newly
  registered).
- From January 1, 2028: All meubles de tourisme must have a DPE rating of at least E
  (F and G properties banned from short-term rental).
- From January 1, 2034: All meubles de tourisme must have a DPE rating of at least D.

These thresholds create a progressive phase-out of energy-inefficient short-term rentals.
Properties that fail to meet the applicable threshold will not be able to obtain or
renew their registration number.

Exception: Properties located in specific zones (e.g., certain overseas territories or
listed historic buildings with technical constraints) may benefit from exemptions or
adapted timelines. Additionally, communes with fewer than 20% secondary residences may
be granted a more flexible timeline by the prefet.

## Step 8: Safety Requirements

### Smoke Detectors (Detecteurs de Fumee)

Since March 8, 2015 (loi ALUR and related decrees), every dwelling in France -- including
meubles de tourisme -- must be equipped with at least one working smoke detector
(detecteur autonome avertisseur de fumee, or DAAF) conforming to the NF EN 14604
standard.

Requirements:
- At least one detector per level of the dwelling.
- Installed preferably in hallways or near sleeping areas.
- Must be maintained in working order (battery checked regularly).

### Carbon Monoxide Detectors (Detecteurs de CO)

While not universally mandated by national law for all dwellings, a carbon monoxide
detector is strongly recommended and may be required in dwellings with combustion
appliances (gas boiler, wood-burning stove, fireplace, etc.). Some commune-level
regulations or insurance policies may require them.

Best practice: Install a CO detector conforming to NF EN 50291 in any property with a
gas, oil, or solid-fuel heating system or fireplace.

### Additional Safety Requirements

Depending on the classification level sought and local regulations:
- Fire extinguisher: Recommended, and sometimes required by copropriete rules or for
  higher-star classifications.
- Emergency information: Display emergency contact numbers (fire: 18, medical: 15,
  police: 17, European emergency: 112) in a visible location.
- Pool safety: If the property has a private swimming pool, it must comply with loi
  n 2003-9 requiring at least one of the four approved safety devices: barrier/fence,
  alarm, cover, or shelter.
- Gas installation certificate: If the property has gas appliances, a certificat de
  conformite for the gas installation may be required.
- Electrical installation: Properties should comply with current electrical safety
  standards (diagnostic electrique).

## Step 9: Tax Obligations

### Income Tax -- Micro-BIC vs. Regime Reel

Rental income from meubles de tourisme is classified as Benefices Industriels et
Commerciaux (BIC) -- commercial income. There are two tax regimes:

#### Micro-BIC Regime (Simplified)

Available when annual gross rental revenue does not exceed the applicable threshold.

Post-Loi Le Meur rates (applicable from 2025 income):
- Classified meubles de tourisme: 50% flat-rate abatement on gross revenue, with a
  revenue ceiling of 77,700 EUR. This is a significant reduction from the previous
  71% abatement.
- Unclassified meubles de tourisme: 30% flat-rate abatement on gross revenue, with a
  revenue ceiling of 15,000 EUR. This is a reduction from the previous 50% abatement
  (with a 77,700 EUR ceiling).

Under micro-BIC, you declare gross revenue and the tax administration automatically
applies the abatement. You pay income tax on the remaining amount at your marginal rate.

Example: An unclassified meuble de tourisme earns 10,000 EUR gross. Under the new rules,
the 30% abatement leaves 7,000 EUR as taxable income.

#### Regime Reel (Actual Expenses)

If your revenue exceeds the micro-BIC thresholds, or if you opt for regime reel (you may
do so voluntarily if it is more advantageous), you deduct actual expenses from revenue:
mortgage interest, insurance, maintenance, depreciation (for regime reel simplifie),
management fees, platform commissions, furniture depreciation, etc.

This regime requires proper accounting and is more complex but can be more favorable for
owners with high expenses (e.g., mortgage interest, major renovations).

### Social Contributions (Cotisations Sociales / Prelevements Sociaux)

- If annual meuble de tourisme revenue is below 23,000 EUR: Revenue is subject to
  prelevements sociaux (social levies) at 17.2% on the taxable portion (after
  abatement under micro-BIC, or on net income under regime reel).
- If annual meuble de tourisme revenue exceeds 23,000 EUR: The operator must register
  with the SSI (Securite Sociale des Independants, formerly RSI) or URSSAF and pay
  social contributions as a non-salaried worker. This applies regardless of whether
  the activity is the owner's primary or secondary occupation.

Since 2017, this threshold has been firmly enforced. Contributions are approximately
20-35% depending on the regime and income level, but they also create social security
entitlements (retirement credits, etc.).

Alternatively, owners earning above 23,000 EUR may register as auto-entrepreneur
(micro-entrepreneur), which simplifies social contribution payments to a fixed
percentage of turnover (approximately 6% for meuble de tourisme under the
micro-entrepreneur regime, subject to change).

### TVA (Value Added Tax)

- Meubles de tourisme are generally exempt from TVA if the operator does not provide
  at least 3 of the 4 para-hotel services (breakfast, regular cleaning during the stay,
  provision of household linen, reception of guests).
- If 3 or more para-hotel services are provided, the activity is subject to TVA at
  10% (reduced rate for accommodation services), and the operator must register for
  TVA.

## Step 10: Platform Reporting Obligations -- DAC7

### What Is DAC7

DAC7 (EU Directive 2021/514) requires digital platforms to collect and report information
about sellers (including short-term rental hosts) to tax authorities. It has been
transposed into French law and is effective from January 1, 2023, with the first reports
submitted in January 2024.

### What Platforms Report

Platforms like Airbnb, Booking.com, and Abritel/Vrbo are required to report the following
to the French tax administration (Direction Generale des Finances Publiques, DGFiP) and
to the host:

- Host identification: Name, address, date of birth, tax identification number (numero
  fiscal), VAT number if applicable.
- Property details: Address of each listed property.
- Financial data: Total revenue paid to the host during the calendar year, number of
  transactions, any fees withheld.

### Host Obligations

- Provide accurate identification and tax information to the platform. Failure to do so
  may result in the platform withholding payouts or suspending listings.
- Declare all rental income on your annual income tax return (declaration de revenus),
  whether or not the platform has reported it.
- Retain records of all rentals for at least 6 years (general tax statute of
  limitations).

## Step 11: Paris-Specific Rules

Paris has the strictest short-term rental regulations in France. Key rules include:

### Registration Number

All meubles de tourisme in Paris must be registered with the Mairie de Paris and display
a 13-digit registration number on all listings. The registration is done online through
the City of Paris website.

### Primary Residence -- 120-Day Limit

If the property is the owner's primary residence, it may be rented for a maximum of 120
days per calendar year. Platforms automatically block bookings beyond 120 days. The
mairie actively monitors compliance and can request rental data from platforms.

### Secondary Residences -- Changement d'Usage + Compensation

If the property is NOT the owner's primary residence (i.e., it is a secondary residence
or an investment property):
- A changement d'usage authorization is mandatory.
- The compensation mechanism requires the owner to convert commercial space to
  residential use. The ratio in most arrondissements is 1:1 of surface area; in
  "reinforced" central arrondissements (1st through 4th, and parts of others), the
  ratio is higher (up to 2:1 or 3:1).
- The authorization is personal (attached to the person, not the property) and
  temporary, typically granted for a renewable period.
- In practice, obtaining changement d'usage in Paris is extremely costly and
  complex, which is by design -- the city aims to limit the conversion of housing stock
  to tourist accommodation.

### Enforcement in Paris

The City of Paris employs dedicated enforcement officers and has imposed millions of
euros in fines on violators since 2017. Enforcement actions include:
- Fines of up to 50,000 EUR per property for failure to register or displaying a
  false registration number.
- Fines of up to 10,000 EUR per day for exceeding the 120-day limit.
- Court orders to return illegally converted properties to residential use.
- Paris has been a pioneer in requesting and receiving rental data from Airbnb and
  other platforms for enforcement purposes.

## Step 12: Sanctions and Fines for Non-Compliance

French law provides for substantial fines, which have been reinforced by the Loi Le Meur:

| Violation                                             | Maximum Fine               |
|-------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Failure to register (no declaration at mairie)        | 10,000 EUR                 |
| Failure to display registration number on listings    | 12,500 EUR                 |
| False or misleading declaration                       | 50,000 EUR                 |
| Exceeding 120-day limit (primary residence)           | 10,000 EUR                 |
| Renting without changement d'usage (where required)   | 50,000 EUR per property    |
| Platform failing to verify/display registration no.   | 12,500 EUR per listing     |
| Platform failing to block bookings beyond 120 days    | 50,000 EUR per listing     |
| Platform failing to transmit data to commune          | 50,000 EUR                 |
| Non-compliance with DPE requirements                  | Registration suspended     |

Additional consequences:
- The mairie may revoke or suspend the registration number.
- Courts may order the restitution of the property to its original residential use.
- Tax authorities may impose back taxes, penalties, and interest on undeclared income.
- Social security agencies (URSSAF) may pursue recovery of unpaid social contributions
  with penalties.

## Step 13: Recent Changes -- Loi Le Meur (Loi n 2024-1039 du 19 novembre 2024)

The Loi Le Meur (named after Deputy Annaig Le Meur) represents the most significant
overhaul of French short-term rental regulation in years. Here is a consolidated summary
of the key changes:

### 1. Universal Registration Obligation
All meubles de tourisme across France must now be declared at the mairie, not just those
in designated communes. A national online registration teleservice is being developed
(expected fully operational by 2026).

### 2. Reduced Tax Advantages
- Classified meubles de tourisme: Micro-BIC abatement reduced from 71% to 50%
  (ceiling 77,700 EUR).
- Unclassified meubles de tourisme: Micro-BIC abatement reduced from 50% to 30%
  (ceiling reduced from 77,700 EUR to 15,000 EUR).
- This aligns the tax treatment of short-term rentals more closely with unfurnished
  long-term rentals.

### 3. DPE Requirements for Short-Term Rentals
Progressive bans on energy-inefficient meubles de tourisme:
- 2025: No new registration of G-rated properties.
- 2028: All F and G-rated properties banned.
- 2034: All E, F, and G-rated properties banned (only D or better allowed).

### 4. Expanded Commune Powers
- Communes may apply changement d'usage rules regardless of population size.
- Communes may set the primary-residence rental cap below 120 days (minimum 90 days).
- Communes may establish geographic quotas and suspension zones for meubles de
  tourisme.
- Communes may require proof of primary-residence status.

### 5. Copropriete Restrictions
- A two-thirds majority vote at the copropriete general assembly is now sufficient to
  amend the reglement de copropriete to ban or restrict meuble de tourisme activity in
  the building (previously required unanimity in many cases).

### 6. Strengthened Platform Obligations
- Platforms must verify and display registration numbers.
- Platforms must block bookings that would exceed the applicable day limit.
- Platforms must transmit rental data to communes upon request.
- Enhanced fines for non-compliant platforms.

### 7. Registration Number Validity and Revocation
- Registration numbers are subject to validity periods and can be suspended or revoked
  by the mairie for violations.
- The mairie may refuse to issue or renew a registration number if the property does
  not meet DPE requirements.

## Compliance Checklist Summary

Before listing your property as a meuble de tourisme in France, ensure you have:

- [ ] Confirmed whether the property is your primary residence or a secondary/investment
      property.
- [ ] Filed the declaration prealable with your local mairie and obtained your numero
      d'enregistrement (registration number).
- [ ] Obtained changement d'usage authorization if required (secondary residence in a
      commune that requires it).
- [ ] Verified that your DPE rating meets the applicable minimum threshold (no G-rated
      properties for new registrations from 2025).
- [ ] Installed working smoke detectors (DAAF) conforming to NF EN 14604.
- [ ] Installed carbon monoxide detectors if the property has combustion appliances.
- [ ] Ensured pool safety compliance if the property has a private pool.
- [ ] Displayed your registration number on all listings and advertisements.
- [ ] Set up taxe de sejour collection (or confirmed your platform handles it).
- [ ] Prepared fiche de police forms for foreign guest registration.
- [ ] Chosen your tax regime (micro-BIC or regime reel) and set up proper record-keeping.
- [ ] Registered with URSSAF/SSI if annual revenue exceeds 23,000 EUR.
- [ ] Provided accurate identification and tax information to your listing platform(s)
      for DAC7 compliance.
- [ ] Checked your copropriete rules (reglement de copropriete) to confirm short-term
      rental is permitted in your building.
- [ ] Obtained optional star classification if desired for tax and marketing advantages.
