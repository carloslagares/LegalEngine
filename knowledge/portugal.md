# Portugal

GUIDE FOR PORTUGAL — ALOJAMENTO LOCAL (AL)
This guide provides a comprehensive, step-by-step overview of the legal and administrative
requirements for operating a short-term rental property (Alojamento Local) in Portugal.
It covers the entire process from initial eligibility to ongoing compliance obligations.

Legal Framework: The Alojamento Local regime is primarily governed by Decreto-Lei
n.o 128/2014, of 29 August, which was subsequently amended by Decreto-Lei n.o 63/2015,
of 23 April, and further modified by Lei n.o 62/2018, of 22 August. The most significant
recent changes came through Lei n.o 56/2023 (the "Mais Habitacao" law), of 6 October 2023,
which introduced substantial restrictions to the AL framework. The regime was further
adjusted by Decreto-Lei n.o 76/2024.

Important Notice: "Mais Habitacao" Law Impact
On 7 October 2023, Lei n.o 56/2023 ("Mais Habitacao" — More Housing) entered into force,
introducing major changes to the AL regime:
- New AL registrations were initially suspended nationwide (a moratorium), though this was
  later lifted in many areas. Municipalities in areas of high housing pressure ("areas de
  pressao urbanistica") retain the power to suspend or limit new registrations.
- Existing AL registrations became subject to a reassessment/revalidation process.
- AL registrations are no longer perpetual; they are now subject to periodic renewal.
- Condominiums received stronger powers to restrict or block AL activity.
- A special "contribution" (contribuicao extraordinaria) on AL properties was introduced.
These changes are reflected throughout this guide.


Step 1: Understand the AL Categories

Portuguese law recognizes four categories of Alojamento Local. You must determine which
category applies to your property:

1. Moradia (House/Villa): An autonomous building, of a single-family typology, used
   entirely for AL accommodation. This includes detached houses, villas, and similar
   standalone structures.

2. Apartamento (Apartment): An autonomous fraction or part of an urban building that
   is used for AL accommodation. This is the most common category for city rentals.

3. Estabelecimento de Hospedagem (Bed & Breakfast / Guesthouse): A unit within a
   building, either a fraction or an entire building, providing accommodation in rooms
   and with shared common areas. This category includes the traditional "Bed & Breakfast"
   model. Establishments with a maximum of 3 rooms and up to 9 guests where the host
   resides may use the designation "Alojamento Local de Hospedagem" and benefit from
   simplified requirements. Hostels are a sub-category (maximum of 4 beds per dormitory
   in residential buildings, or more beds if not in residential buildings).

4. Quartos (Rooms): Rooms rented within the owner's or tenant's own primary residence,
   with a maximum of 3 rooms. The host must be domiciled at the property. This is the
   simplest category with the lightest regulatory burden.

Capacity Limits:
- Moradia: Maximum 9 rooms and 30 guests.
- Apartamento: Maximum 9 rooms and 30 guests.
- Estabelecimento de Hospedagem: Maximum 9 rooms and 30 guests (unless it is a hostel).
- Quartos: Maximum 3 rooms.


Step 2: Check Condominium and Local Restrictions

Before proceeding, verify that short-term rental activity is permitted at your property:

Condominium Rules (Lei n.o 56/2023 amendments):
- If the property is in a building with a condominium (propriedade horizontal), the owners'
  assembly (assembleia de condominios) may oppose AL activity.
- Under the Mais Habitacao law, the condominium assembly can vote to oppose a new AL
  registration or request cancellation of an existing one with a majority of more than
  50% of the permilagem (ownership share), which effectively corresponds to a simple
  majority of the capital. Previously, this required a two-thirds majority.
- Earlier legislation (Lei n.o 62/2018) had already allowed condominiums to oppose AL
  with a vote of more than two-thirds (2/3) of the ownership share. The 2023 law lowered
  this threshold.
- Check the condominium regulations (regulamento do condominio) for any existing
  restrictions on commercial or tourism-related use.
- It is strongly recommended to notify the condominium administrator and obtain minutes
  confirming there is no opposition before registering.

Municipal Restrictions:
- Municipalities, particularly those in high-density urban areas (zonas de pressao
  urbanistica), may impose additional restrictions, caps, or suspensions on new AL
  registrations.
- Lisbon and Porto have been particularly active in implementing area-based restrictions,
  including caps on the ratio of AL units to total residential units in certain parishes
  (freguesias).
- Check with the local Camara Municipal (town hall) for any specific local regulations,
  zoning restrictions, or moratoriums in effect.


Step 3: Ensure Technical and Safety Compliance

Your property must meet specific technical requirements before registration:

General Requirements (all categories):
- The property must have adequate conditions of conservation, hygiene, and functionality.
- Must have a water supply system (hot and cold), drainage, and electricity.
- Must comply with applicable building safety regulations.

Fire Safety:
- Fire extinguisher(s) accessible and properly maintained (minimum 1 per floor).
- Fire blanket in the kitchen area.
- First-aid kit.
- Emergency lighting in corridors and exits (for multi-room establishments).
- Emergency evacuation plan displayed in a visible location, in Portuguese and English
  (minimum). For estabelecimentos de hospedagem, additional fire safety measures per
  building size may be required.
- Smoke detectors are recommended (and increasingly required by municipal regulations).
- Fire safety requirements are governed by the Regime Juridico de Seguranca contra
  Incendios em Edificios (Decreto-Lei n.o 220/2008 and Portaria n.o 1532/2008), with
  specific simplified rules for AL in the relevant portarias.

Furnishing and Equipment:
- Adequate furniture for the capacity declared (beds, wardrobes/storage, table, seating).
- Bedding and towels (or a clear policy that guests provide their own).
- Ventilation: Windows or adequate ventilation in all habitable rooms and bathrooms.
- Bathroom facilities with running water.
- Cleaning of the unit between guest stays.
- Information to guests: House rules, emergency numbers, operating instructions for
  equipment, and a complaints book (Livro de Reclamacoes — mandatory for all AL).

Accessibility:
- Properties on the ground floor or with elevator access should consider accessibility
  requirements, though this is not universally mandatory for all existing AL.

Signage:
- An AL identification plaque must be displayed at the entrance of the property.
  The plaque follows the official model and includes the AL registration number.


Step 4: Obtain Required Documentation

Before submitting your registration, gather the following:

- Caderneta Predial (property tax record) or certidao da Conservatoria do Registo Predial
  (land registry certificate) — to prove ownership or legitimate use of the property.
- Title deed or lease agreement — if you are not the owner, you need written authorization
  from the landlord permitting AL activity.
- Declaration of compliance with the maximum capacity (number of guests).
- Civil liability insurance policy (see Step 6).
- Term of responsibility for fire safety compliance, signed by a qualified technician
  (for estabelecimentos de hospedagem and larger operations).
- Condominium authorization or evidence of no opposition (see Step 2).


Step 5: Register on the RNAL (Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local)

The main registration is done through the Balcao Unico Eletronico (single electronic
counter), which feeds into the RNAL — the national register of local accommodation.

Process:
1. Access the online portal at https://eportugal.gov.pt (the Balcao do Empreendedor).
2. Submit a "Mera Comunicacao Previa" (prior communication) — this is not a license
   application but rather a declaration that you are commencing AL activity and that you
   comply with all legal requirements. As of the Mais Habitacao changes, this was converted
   to a "comunicacao previa com prazo" (prior communication with a deadline) for
   the Camara Municipal to respond.
3. Include all required documentation:
   - Identification of the owner/operator (NIF — tax identification number).
   - Property identification (address, caderneta predial reference, or matricial article).
   - Declaration of the AL category and maximum capacity.
   - Proof of civil liability insurance.
   - Term of responsibility for fire safety.
   - Authorization from the property owner (if applicable).
   - Condominium authorization or declaration of no opposition.
4. The Camara Municipal (local town hall) has a legally defined period to oppose the
   registration or request an inspection. Under the Mais Habitacao law, the municipality
   has a period (typically up to 60 days under the current framework) to conduct an
   inspection or raise objections. If the municipality does not respond within this period,
   the registration may proceed.
5. Upon successful registration, you receive the RNAL registration number, which must be
   displayed on all advertisements, listings, and the property itself.

RNAL Number Format:
- The registration number is typically in the format XXXXX/AL, and must appear in all
  online listings (Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.) and marketing materials.

Cost:
- There is no national registration fee for the RNAL itself, but municipalities may charge
  fees for inspections or administrative processing.

Validity and Renewal (post-Mais Habitacao):
- AL registrations are no longer perpetual. They are subject to periodic renewal
  (the specific renewal period has been set at 5 years in the current framework).
- Existing registrations were required to undergo a revalidation process.
- Failure to renew results in automatic cancellation of the registration.


Step 6: Insurance Requirements

Civil liability insurance is mandatory for all AL properties:

- You must hold a valid seguro de responsabilidade civil (civil liability insurance) that
  covers damages to guests and third parties arising from the AL activity.
- The insurance must be in force at all times while the property is registered and active.
- While the law does not specify a minimum coverage amount, insurers typically offer
  policies starting at EUR 75,000 to EUR 150,000, with higher amounts recommended for
  larger properties.
- Multi-risk property insurance (covering fire, water damage, theft, etc.) is strongly
  recommended but not legally mandatory specifically for AL (though it may be required
  by your mortgage lender or condominium).


Step 7: Fiscal Obligations

Operating an AL has significant tax implications in Portugal:

Income Tax (IRS — Imposto sobre o Rendimento de Pessoas Singulares):
- AL income is declared under Category B (rendimentos empresariais e profissionais —
  business and professional income) of the IRS.
- You must register the activity with the Autoridade Tributaria (Tax Authority) under
  CAE 55201 (Alojamento Local) or the equivalent CIRS code.
- Two regimes are available:
  a) Regime Simplificado (Simplified Regime): Available if annual gross income does not
     exceed EUR 200,000. A coefficient is applied to determine the taxable income:
     - For Moradia and Apartamento: 35% of gross income is considered taxable
       (i.e., a 65% automatic deduction). However, under the Mais Habitacao law,
       the coefficient was increased to 50% for new registrations or in certain
       circumstances, reducing the effective deduction to 50%.
     - For Estabelecimento de Hospedagem: 35% coefficient (standard).
     - For Quartos: Income may qualify for a reduced coefficient.
  b) Contabilidade Organizada (Organized Bookkeeping): Mandatory if annual income exceeds
     EUR 200,000, or optional below that threshold. Allows deduction of actual expenses
     (mortgage interest, utilities, maintenance, depreciation, etc.) against income.
     Requires a certified accountant (contabilista certificado/TOC).
- Quarterly advance payments (pagamentos por conta) may be required based on prior-year
  income.

VAT (IVA — Imposto sobre o Valor Acrescentado):
- AL activity is subject to VAT. If you are in the simplified regime and your annual
  turnover does not exceed EUR 14,500 (2024 threshold — this may be adjusted), you
  benefit from VAT exemption under Article 53 of the CIVA.
- If above the exemption threshold, VAT at the reduced rate of 6% applies to
  accommodation services (applicable rate for mainland Portugal; Azores and Madeira
  have different reduced rates).

Social Security:
- If AL is your primary activity, you may be required to contribute to Social Security
  as a self-employed worker (trabalhador independente).

Contribuicao Extraordinaria sobre o Alojamento Local (CEAL):
- Introduced by the Mais Habitacao law, this is an extraordinary contribution levied on
  AL properties located in areas of high urban pressure. The CEAL is calculated based on
  applicable coefficients and the property's tax value (VPT — valor patrimonial tributario).
  The specific rates and applicable areas are defined by regulation.

Municipal Property Tax (IMI):
- Standard IMI applies. Some municipalities may apply a surcharge (derrama) or
  differentiated rates for properties used as AL.


Step 8: Guest Registration — SEF/SIBA/SES System

All AL operators are legally required to report guest data to the authorities:

Legal Basis: Lei n.o 23/2007 (immigration law) and related regulations require all
accommodation providers to report foreign guest data. Decreto-Lei n.o 128/2014 extends
this to all AL.

Current System — SES.Hospedagens (formerly SIBA):
- The guest registration obligation was historically handled through the SEF (Servico de
  Estrangeiros e Fronteiras — Foreigners and Borders Service) via the SIBA system
  (Sistema Integrado de Boletins de Alojamento).
- The SEF was dissolved in October 2023 (also under the Mais Habitacao reform package),
  and its functions were transferred to AIMA (Agencia para a Integracao, Migracoes e Asilo)
  and security forces (PSP/GNR).
- The guest registration function migrated to the SES.Hospedagens platform, managed by
  the security services.
- As of 2025, Spain and Portugal's systems are converging around the broader EU framework
  for guest data reporting.

What You Must Report:
- For every guest, you must submit a boletim de alojamento (accommodation bulletin)
  within 3 working days of check-in (the deadline may vary — some interpretations
  require reporting within 24 hours).
- Data required: Full name, nationality, date of birth, identification document type
  and number, date of entry, and expected date of departure.
- Portuguese citizens and EU nationals must also be registered.

How to Report:
1. Register on the SES.Hospedagens platform (https://sfrh.ses.pt or the current
   active URL) using your AL registration number and NIF.
2. For each guest stay, submit the guest bulletin electronically through the platform.
3. Retain records for a minimum period as required by law.

Penalties for Non-Compliance:
- Failure to register guests or late registration can result in fines and potential
  suspension of the AL registration.


Step 9: Tourist Tax (Taxa Turistica)

Several Portuguese municipalities levy a tourist tax on overnight stays:

Lisbon:
- EUR 2.00 per guest per night, for a maximum of 7 consecutive nights per stay.
- Applicable to guests aged 13 and over.
- The tax must be collected by the AL operator and remitted to the Camara Municipal
  de Lisboa.
- Payment and reporting through the municipality's dedicated platform.

Porto:
- EUR 2.00 per guest per night, for a maximum of 7 consecutive nights.
- Applicable to guests aged 13 and over.
- Collected by the operator and remitted to the Camara Municipal do Porto.

Other Municipalities:
- Several other municipalities have introduced or are considering tourist taxes, including
  Vila Nova de Gaia, Cascais, Faro, and municipalities in the Algarve.
- Rates and conditions vary; always check with the specific Camara Municipal.

Important:
- The tourist tax is separate from and additional to VAT and income tax obligations.
- The tax must be itemized or disclosed to guests at the time of booking or check-in.
- Failure to collect and remit the tourist tax results in municipal fines.


Step 10: Ongoing Operational Obligations

Once your AL is registered and operational, you must maintain continuous compliance:

Advertising and Listings:
- All advertisements, online listings, and marketing materials must display the RNAL
  registration number prominently.
- This applies to platforms such as Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO, and any personal websites
  or social media promotion.
- Platforms are increasingly required to verify and display registration numbers under
  EU Digital Services Act requirements.

Complaints Book (Livro de Reclamacoes):
- You must have a physical complaints book (Livro de Reclamacoes) available at the
  property and inform guests of its availability.
- An electronic complaints book option is also available through the portal
  www.livroreclamacoes.pt.

Record Keeping:
- Maintain records of all guest stays, income, and expenses for the legally required
  period (generally 4 years for tax purposes, though some obligations extend further).

Property Standards:
- The property must remain in compliance with all technical, safety, and habitability
  standards throughout the period of registration.
- The Camara Municipal may conduct inspections at any time.

Changes:
- Any change to the property characteristics, capacity, category, ownership, or operator
  must be communicated to the RNAL and the Camara Municipal.
- Transfer of the AL registration to a new owner or operator requires specific procedures.


Step 11: Sanctions and Fines

Non-compliance with AL regulations carries significant penalties:

Operating Without Registration:
- Operating an AL without a valid RNAL registration constitutes a contravencao
  (administrative offense) punishable by fines ranging from approximately EUR 2,500
  to EUR 4,000 for individuals and EUR 25,000 to EUR 40,000 for legal entities
  (companies).
- The property may be sealed and the activity immediately suspended.

Other Infractions:
- Failure to display the registration number in advertisements: fines from EUR 250
  to EUR 3,500.
- Failure to comply with capacity limits: fines from EUR 2,500 to EUR 25,000.
- Failure to meet safety and technical requirements: fines from EUR 250 to EUR 3,500,
  with potential suspension of activity.
- Failure to register guests with authorities: fines under the applicable security
  legislation.
- Failure to collect/remit tourist tax: municipal fines as defined by each municipality.

Cancellation of Registration:
- The Camara Municipal may cancel an AL registration in cases of:
  - Serious or repeated non-compliance.
  - Condominium opposition (by the required majority vote).
  - False declarations in the registration process.
  - Failure to renew within the required timeframe.
  - Persistent guest complaints or public order issues.

Accessory Sanctions:
- In addition to fines, accessory sanctions may include temporary or permanent prohibition
  of the activity and public disclosure of the infraction.


Summary of Key Legal References

- Decreto-Lei n.o 128/2014, of 29 August — Establishes the AL regime.
- Decreto-Lei n.o 63/2015, of 23 April — First amendment to the AL regime.
- Lei n.o 62/2018, of 22 August — Second amendment (condominium rules, municipal powers).
- Lei n.o 56/2023, of 6 October — "Mais Habitacao" (More Housing) law — major overhaul
  of AL rules including moratorium, renewal requirements, CEAL, and condominium changes.
- Decreto-Lei n.o 76/2024 — Further adjustments to the AL framework.
- Decreto-Lei n.o 220/2008 and Portaria n.o 1532/2008 — Fire safety regime (RJSCIE).
- Lei n.o 23/2007 — Immigration law (guest reporting obligations).
- Codigo do IRS — Income tax code (Category B income).
- Codigo do IVA (CIVA) — VAT code (Article 53 exemption, 6% reduced rate).
