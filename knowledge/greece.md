# Greece

COMPREHENSIVE VACATION RENTAL COMPLIANCE GUIDE FOR GREECE

This guide covers all regulatory requirements for operating a short-term rental
(Βραχυχρόνια Μίσθωση) in Greece. It is written in a step-by-step format for
property owners and managers who wish to list properties on platforms such as
Airbnb, Booking.com, or VRBO.

**Legal framework**: Law 4446/2016 (Articles 111-114), Law 4472/2017 (Article 84),
Law 4936/2022, Joint Ministerial Decisions, and subsequent amendments through
2024-2025. The Independent Authority for Public Revenue (AADE - Ανεξάρτητη Αρχή
Δημοσίων Εσόδων) is the primary regulatory authority.

> **Note**: Web-based verification of 2025-2026 changes was not possible at the
> time of writing. Hosts should verify the latest rules on the official AADE
> portal (aade.gr) and consult a Greek tax advisor for current requirements.

---

## Step 0: Preliminary Check — "Quick Folder"

Before starting any registration process, gather the following:

- **Tax Identification Number (AFM / ΑΦΜ)**: Greek tax number. Non-residents
  must obtain one from the competent tax office or appoint a tax representative.
- **TAXISnet credentials**: Login for the AADE online platform (aade.gr).
- **Property details**: Full address, cadastral reference (KAEK), floor, size
  in square metres, number of rooms and beds.
- **Proof of legal right to lease**: Ownership deed, or lease agreement
  permitting sub-letting, or property management authorisation.
- **Energy Performance Certificate (PEA / ΠΕΑ)**: Required for the property.
- **Building permit or legality documentation**: Proof the property is legally
  constructed or has been regularised under applicable laws.
- **Community of owners rules**: Confirm that the condominium regulations (if
  applicable) do not prohibit short-term rental activity.

## Step 1: Register on the Short-Term Rental Registry (AADE)

### The Registry

Greece operates the **Short-Term Rental Property Registry** (Μητρώο Ακινήτων
Βραχυχρόνιας Μίσθωσης), managed by AADE through its digital platform. Every
property offered for short-term rental must be registered here before it can be
advertised or rented.

### How to register

1. Log in to the AADE digital platform at **aade.gr** using your TAXISnet
   credentials.
2. Navigate to the short-term rental section (Βραχυχρόνια Μίσθωση).
3. Enter the property details:
   - Property address, floor, and description.
   - KAEK (cadastral number) or ATAK (property tax reference number from E9).
   - Property type (apartment, house, room, etc.).
   - Maximum capacity (number of guests).
   - Your legal relationship to the property (owner, lessee, manager).
4. Submit the application.
5. You will receive a **Property Registration Number (Αριθμός Μητρώου Ακινήτου
   / AMA)**.

### Property Registration Number (AMA)

- The AMA is a unique identifier assigned to each registered short-term rental
  property.
- It is **mandatory** to display the AMA in every advertisement, listing, or
  promotional material for the property, whether online or offline.
- Platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.) are required to display the AMA on
  listings and may block listings that lack a valid AMA.
- Each property receives its own AMA; if you have multiple properties, each
  must be registered separately.

## Step 2: Understand the Maximum Days Limits

Greek law imposes annual caps on the number of days a property can be rented
on a short-term basis. The limits depend on the host's status and the property
location.

### General rules

| Host category | Annual day limit per property |
|---|---|
| Non-professional host (up to 2 properties) | **90 days** per calendar year |
| Non-professional host in areas with special restrictions | **60 days** per calendar year |
| Professional host (registered business activity) | **No annual cap** (but must hold a business licence and meet additional obligations) |

### Key details

- The **90-day limit** applies to each individual property, not to the host
  in aggregate. If you own two properties, each one may be rented for up to
  90 days.
- The **60-day limit** applies in areas where the government has identified
  housing pressure or overtourism concerns. These areas are designated by
  ministerial decision and have historically included certain islands and
  popular tourist zones. Hosts should check current designations on the AADE
  portal.
- If a non-professional host exceeds these limits, the income is treated as
  business income, which triggers additional tax and social security obligations.
- To operate beyond these day limits, a host must register as a professional
  short-term rental operator, which requires:
  - Registering a business activity (start of business notification at the tax
    office).
  - Obtaining a special operation mark/licence (Ειδικό Σήμα Λειτουργίας) or
    meeting the equivalent modern registration requirements.
  - Complying with all professional operator obligations (VAT, bookkeeping,
    social security, etc.).

### 2024-2025 regulatory tightening

The Greek government introduced additional restrictions in 2024-2025 to address
housing availability concerns, particularly in Athens and popular islands:

- Discussions and proposals for further reducing the day cap in high-demand
  urban areas (e.g., parts of Athens centre).
- Potential requirement for minimum distances between short-term rental
  properties in certain zones.
- Enhanced enforcement through cross-referencing platform data with AADE
  records.
- Municipalities gained more powers to impose local restrictions or temporary
  moratoriums on new short-term rental registrations in specific neighbourhoods.

Hosts should monitor official announcements from the Ministry of Tourism and
AADE for the latest applicable limits in their area.

## Step 3: Guest Reporting to Police

### Obligation

All short-term rental hosts in Greece are legally required to report guest
information to the Greek Police (Ελληνική Αστυνομία). This applies to both
Greek and foreign guests.

### How it works

1. **Collect guest data upon arrival**: For every guest, you must record:
   - Full name.
   - Nationality.
   - Date of birth.
   - Passport or ID card number.
   - Date of arrival and expected departure.

2. **Submit to police**: Guest data must be transmitted to the local police
   authority. This is done through:
   - The online police reporting platform (guest registration system operated
     by the Hellenic Police).
   - In some cases, physical submission at the local police station is still
     accepted, but electronic submission is increasingly required.

3. **Timing**: Guest information should be reported **within 24 hours** of the
   guest's arrival (or on the same day, depending on the specific local
   requirements).

4. **Record keeping**: Hosts must maintain a guest register (police book /
   αστυνομικό βιβλίο) with all guest data for a period determined by law
   (typically at least one year).

### Penalties for non-compliance

Failure to report guests to the police can result in administrative fines and
potential criminal liability. This obligation is separate from the AADE
registration requirements.

## Step 4: Tax Obligations

### Income tax on rental income

Short-term rental income is subject to Greek income tax. The tax treatment
depends on whether the host operates as a non-professional or professional.

#### Non-professional hosts (income from property / εισόδημα ακινήτων)

Short-term rental income for non-professional hosts is taxed under the
property income scale (same as long-term rental income):

| Taxable rental income (EUR) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 - 12,000 | 15% |
| 12,001 - 35,000 | 35% |
| 35,001 and above | 45% |

- A **solidarity surcharge** may also apply (although it was suspended for
  private sector income in recent years; hosts should check the current status).
- Rental income is declared annually through the personal income tax return
  (Form E1) and the rental income annex (Form E2).

#### Professional hosts (business income)

If the host exceeds the day limits or registers as a professional operator:

- Income is treated as **business income** and taxed at the applicable business
  income tax rates.
- **VAT** may apply (standard rate 24%, reduced rate 13% for accommodation
  services — check current applicable rate for short-term rentals).
- The host must issue invoices and maintain proper books and records.
- Social security (EFKA) contributions apply.

### Declaration of short-term rental income to AADE

In addition to the annual tax return, hosts must submit a **Short-Term Rental
Income Declaration** through the AADE platform:

- Each rental transaction must be declared individually on the AADE platform,
  typically within the month following the rental period.
- The declaration includes: guest details, rental dates, rental amount, and
  the property AMA.
- AADE cross-references these declarations with platform data and tax returns.

### Withholding / platform reporting

Platforms may be required to withhold tax at source or report host income
directly to AADE, in line with EU DAC7 requirements (see Step 9 below).

## Step 5: E-Invoicing through myDATA

### What is myDATA?

myDATA (my Digital Accounting and Tax Application) is Greece's mandatory
electronic books and invoicing platform, operated by AADE. It requires
businesses to transmit their revenue and expense data electronically in
real time.

### Applicability to short-term rentals

- **Professional hosts** (those with a registered business activity) are
  required to use myDATA. All income from short-term rentals must be
  transmitted to the myDATA platform.
- **Non-professional hosts** renting up to 2 properties within the day limits
  are generally not required to use myDATA, as their income is classified as
  property income rather than business income. However, they must still
  declare their income through the AADE short-term rental platform and their
  annual tax return.
- If a non-professional host crosses the threshold into professional activity
  (exceeds day limits, operates more than 2-3 properties, etc.), myDATA
  obligations are triggered.

### How myDATA works for professional hosts

1. Each rental transaction must be recorded and transmitted to myDATA.
2. Revenue classifications must be assigned correctly (accommodation services).
3. Transmission can be done through:
   - Accounting software integrated with the myDATA API.
   - The myDATA web portal on AADE.
   - Through the host's accountant.
4. Invoices or revenue summaries must be issued for each booking and
   transmitted in real time or within the applicable deadline.

## Step 6: Tourist Tax / Accommodation Duty

### Overview

Greece imposes a **tourist accommodation duty** (τέλος διαμονής / Accommodation
Duty) on overnight stays. This applies to hotels, serviced apartments, and
short-term rental properties.

### Rates

The tourist tax for short-term rental properties (apartments/houses listed on
platforms) is typically set at a flat rate per room per night:

| Property category | Rate per room per night |
|---|---|
| 1-2 star hotels | EUR 0.50 |
| 3 star hotels | EUR 1.50 |
| 4 star hotels | EUR 3.00 |
| 5 star hotels | EUR 4.00 |
| Rental rooms / apartments (short-term rentals) | EUR 0.50 per room per night |

- The applicable rate for short-term rental properties (non-hotel) is at the
  lowest tier, typically **EUR 0.50 per room per night**.
- The tax is collected from the guest by the host.
- The host must remit the collected tax to the tax authorities.
- The tax is declared and paid through the AADE platform, typically on a
  periodic basis (monthly or quarterly).

### Important notes

- The tourist tax applies year-round.
- It is the host's responsibility to collect and remit the tax; failure to do
  so results in fines.
- Some municipalities may impose additional local fees; hosts should check
  with their local municipality.
- Recent legislative discussions have considered increasing these rates,
  particularly for high-demand areas. Check current rates on AADE.

## Step 7: Safety and Quality Requirements

### Mandatory property standards

Short-term rental properties in Greece must meet minimum safety and quality
standards:

#### Safety

- **Fire safety**: Properties must have functional smoke detectors and fire
  extinguishers. Multi-storey buildings must have clearly marked emergency
  exits and evacuation plans.
- **Gas safety**: Gas installations must comply with Greek technical standards
  and be regularly inspected.
- **Electrical safety**: Electrical installations must be safe and compliant
  with regulations. Properties should have properly functioning circuit
  breakers.
- **Carbon monoxide detectors**: Recommended (and increasingly required) for
  properties with gas heating or cooking appliances.
- **First aid kit**: A basic first aid kit should be available.
- **Emergency information**: Emergency contact numbers (police, ambulance,
  fire department) must be clearly displayed.

#### Quality and equipment

- The property must be **fully furnished** and equipped for immediate
  habitation.
- Required amenities include:
  - Heating and/or air conditioning (depending on the season and location).
  - Hot water.
  - Clean bedding and towels.
  - Functioning kitchen or kitchenette (for apartments/houses).
  - Adequate bathroom facilities.
  - Internet/WiFi (increasingly expected, though not always legally mandated).
- The property must meet **hygiene standards** — professional cleaning between
  guests is expected.
- **Accessibility**: While not universally mandated for small private rentals,
  hosts should be aware of any local accessibility requirements.

#### Building legality

- The property must have a valid building permit or have been regularised
  under applicable amnesty laws.
- Properties in illegal or semi-legal structures cannot be registered for
  short-term rental.

## Step 8: Insurance Requirements

### Recommended and required coverage

- **Civil liability insurance**: While not always explicitly mandated by law
  for non-professional hosts, it is strongly recommended and may be required
  by platforms. This covers damage or injury to guests during their stay.
- **Property insurance**: Standard property insurance covering fire, natural
  disasters, theft, and water damage.
- **Professional liability insurance**: Required for professional operators
  and property management companies.
- **Platform insurance**: Airbnb and some other platforms provide host
  protection insurance (e.g., AirCover), but this should not be relied upon
  as the sole coverage. Hosts should maintain their own independent insurance.

### Best practices

- Inform your insurance provider that the property is used for short-term
  rentals, as standard homeowner policies may not cover commercial use.
- Ensure coverage includes guest injuries and property damage caused by guests.
- Consider business interruption insurance if rental income is significant.

## Step 9: Restrictions on Islands and Specific Areas

### Island and high-demand area restrictions

The Greek government has taken steps to address overtourism and housing
shortages in popular destinations:

#### Reduced day limits

- Certain islands and popular tourist areas are subject to the **60-day annual
  cap** instead of the standard 90 days for non-professional hosts.
- The specific areas subject to this restriction are designated by ministerial
  decision and can change. Historically affected areas have included popular
  Cycladic islands (e.g., Santorini, Mykonos) and other high-demand locations.

#### Athens-specific measures

- The centre of Athens has been subject to increased scrutiny and potential
  additional restrictions on short-term rentals.
- In 2024-2025, the government proposed or implemented measures to limit the
  concentration of short-term rentals in specific Athens neighbourhoods to
  protect housing availability for residents.
- Potential caps on the percentage of properties in a building or area that
  can be used as short-term rentals.

#### Other location-specific rules

- **Archaeological zones**: Properties near protected archaeological sites
  may face additional restrictions on signage, exterior modifications, or
  usage.
- **Protected natural areas**: Properties in Natura 2000 zones or other
  protected areas may have additional environmental compliance requirements.
- **Traditional settlements**: Properties in designated traditional
  settlements (παραδοσιακοί οικισμοί) may face restrictions on exterior
  modifications.

### Municipal powers

Greek municipalities have the authority to:
- Request data on short-term rental properties in their jurisdiction.
- Propose or implement local restrictions.
- Conduct inspections.
- Impose local fees or levies.

## Step 10: Platform Reporting Obligations

### EU DAC7 Directive implementation

Greece has transposed the EU DAC7 Directive (Council Directive 2021/514),
which requires digital platforms to:

1. **Collect host information**: Platforms must collect and verify the identity,
   tax identification number (AFM), and address of all hosts.
2. **Report to tax authorities**: Platforms must report host income data
   annually to AADE, including:
   - Total consideration paid or credited to the host.
   - Number of transactions.
   - Property address.
   - Number of rental days.
   - Host identification details.
3. **Cross-border reporting**: Platform data is shared between EU tax
   authorities, meaning income earned through platforms in Greece is visible
   to all EU member state tax authorities.

### AADE property number requirement on platforms

- Platforms operating in Greece are required to ensure that listings display
  a valid AMA (Property Registration Number).
- Platforms may block or remove listings that do not provide a valid AMA.
- AADE can request platforms to remove non-compliant listings.

### Host obligations regarding platforms

- Hosts must provide their AMA to every platform where they list the property.
- Hosts must ensure their tax information on platforms is accurate and
  up-to-date.
- Hosts should be aware that platform-reported income is cross-referenced
  with their tax declarations.

## Step 11: Sanctions and Fines

### Administrative fines

Non-compliance with short-term rental regulations in Greece can result in
significant penalties:

| Violation | Fine |
|---|---|
| Operating without registration (no AMA) | EUR 5,000 per property |
| Failure to display AMA on listings | EUR 5,000 per listing |
| Failure to submit rental declarations to AADE | EUR 5,000 per undeclared rental |
| Exceeding the annual day limit | EUR 5,000 + reclassification as professional income |
| Failure to report guests to police | Administrative fines + potential criminal penalties |
| Failure to collect/remit tourist tax | Fines equal to the unpaid tax + surcharges |
| Tax evasion (unreported income) | Tax penalties of 50%-120% of unpaid tax + interest |
| Repeat offences | Double fines; potential suspension of AMA |

### Enforcement mechanisms

- **AADE audits**: AADE conducts cross-checks between platform data, rental
  declarations, and tax returns to identify undeclared income.
- **Platform data sharing**: Under DAC7, AADE receives comprehensive data
  from all major platforms.
- **Municipal inspections**: Local authorities can inspect properties for
  compliance with safety, quality, and registration requirements.
- **Neighbour complaints**: Complaints from neighbours or the community of
  owners can trigger inspections.
- **Suspension or revocation of AMA**: In serious or repeat cases, AADE can
  suspend or revoke the property's registration number, effectively
  prohibiting the host from operating.

## Step 12: Recent 2024-2025 Regulatory Changes

The Greek government significantly tightened short-term rental regulations
in 2024 and 2025. Key changes include:

### 1. Stricter enforcement and platform integration

- Enhanced data exchange between AADE and digital platforms to identify
  unregistered properties and unreported income.
- Implementation of DAC7 reporting from January 2024 onwards.
- Automated cross-referencing systems to detect discrepancies between
  platform-reported revenue and declared income.

### 2. Housing protection measures

- Introduction of potential caps on the number of short-term rental
  properties in specific areas experiencing housing pressure, particularly
  in Athens city centre.
- Municipal authorities granted expanded powers to regulate or restrict
  short-term rentals in their jurisdictions.
- Discussion of proposals requiring a minimum ratio of long-term to
  short-term rental properties in certain buildings or zones.

### 3. Tax changes

- Continued focus on ensuring all short-term rental income is properly
  declared and taxed.
- Strengthened penalties for undeclared rental income.
- Integration of short-term rental income reporting with the broader myDATA
  electronic invoicing ecosystem for professional hosts.

### 4. Safety and quality standards

- Increased emphasis on fire safety compliance, particularly following
  high-profile incidents.
- Proposals for mandatory safety inspections for short-term rental
  properties.
- Discussion of a quality rating or certification system for short-term
  rentals.

### 5. Island and overtourism management

- Ongoing review of the 60-day reduced cap areas, with potential expansion
  to additional islands and popular destinations.
- Enhanced coordination between the Ministry of Tourism, AADE, and municipal
  authorities in managing short-term rental density.

## Checklist — Final Compliance Summary

1. [ ] Obtain a Greek Tax Number (AFM) and TAXISnet credentials.
2. [ ] Gather all property documentation (deed, building permit, energy
       certificate).
3. [ ] Register the property on the AADE Short-Term Rental Registry and
       obtain the AMA.
4. [ ] Display the AMA on all listings and advertisements.
5. [ ] Verify the applicable day limit for your area (90 days or 60 days).
6. [ ] Set up guest reporting to the Greek Police (online system or local
       station).
7. [ ] Understand your tax obligations: declare rental income on Forms E1/E2
       and through the AADE short-term rental platform.
8. [ ] If operating professionally: register with myDATA, issue invoices,
       register for VAT if required, and pay EFKA contributions.
9. [ ] Collect and remit the tourist accommodation duty (EUR 0.50 per room
       per night for standard short-term rentals).
10. [ ] Ensure the property meets fire safety and quality standards (smoke
        detectors, fire extinguisher, clean bedding, functioning utilities).
11. [ ] Obtain appropriate insurance coverage (civil liability at minimum).
12. [ ] Check for location-specific restrictions (islands, Athens, protected
        areas).
13. [ ] Provide accurate tax and identification information to all platforms.
14. [ ] Keep records of all bookings, guest data, income, and tax payments
        for at least 5 years.
15. [ ] Monitor AADE and Ministry of Tourism announcements for regulatory
        changes.
