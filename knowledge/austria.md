# Austria

GUIDE FOR AUSTRIA -- VACATION RENTAL COMPLIANCE
This guide is intended to orient property owners and managers on the administrative,
legal, and tax requirements for operating a short-term vacation rental
(Ferienwohnung / Ferienunterkunft) in Austria. Because Austria is a federal republic
of nine Bundeslaender (states), many rules vary by region. This guide covers
federal-level obligations and highlights the most important regional differences.

Disclaimer: This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute
legal advice. Austrian regulations change frequently and vary by municipality.
Always verify current requirements with local authorities (Bezirkshauptmannschaft,
Magistrat, or Gemeinde) and consult a Steuerberater (tax advisor) for tax matters.

---

## Step 1: Understand the Legal Framework

Austrian vacation rental regulation operates on three levels:

### 1.1 Federal Level
- **Gewerbeordnung (GewO) 1994** -- the Federal Trade Regulation Act. Renting
  furnished accommodation to tourists on a recurring, commercial basis can
  constitute a "freies Gewerbe" (unregulated trade) requiring a
  Gewerbeanmeldung (trade registration). However, the distinction between
  private asset management (Vermoegensverwaltung) and a commercial trade
  depends on the scope of services provided.
- **Meldegesetz 1991** -- Federal Registration Act. All accommodation providers
  must register guests (Meldepflicht).
- **Bundesabgabenordnung (BAO)** and federal tax laws -- income tax and VAT
  obligations.
- **DAC7 implementation** (EU Directive 2021/514) -- platform reporting
  obligations, transposed into Austrian law.

### 1.2 State (Bundesland) Level
Each of the nine states has its own:
- **Tourismusgesetz** (Tourism Act) -- defines categories of accommodation,
  registration with tourism associations (Tourismusverband), and tourist tax
  (Ortstaxe/Naechtigungstaxe).
- **Raumordnungsgesetz** (Spatial Planning Act) -- zoning rules that determine
  whether short-term rental is permitted in a given area.
- **Bauordnung** (Building Code) -- building permits and usage change
  requirements.

### 1.3 Municipal Level
- Individual municipalities (Gemeinden) may impose additional restrictions,
  zoning overlays, or local bylaws (Verordnungen) affecting vacation rentals.

### Key Distinction: Private vs. Commercial Rental
- **Private asset management (Vermoegensverwaltung):** If you rent out one or
  two properties without providing hotel-like services (no breakfast, no daily
  cleaning, no reception), this is typically treated as private rental income,
  not a trade. No Gewerbeanmeldung is required.
- **Commercial accommodation (Gewerbliche Beherbergung):** If you provide
  additional services (meals, daily housekeeping, concierge, organized
  activities), or operate at a scale suggesting commercial activity (multiple
  units, professional marketing), a trade registration is required.
- The threshold is assessed case-by-case. Austrian case law (VwGH rulings)
  generally holds that merely furnishing a property and offering it on
  platforms like Airbnb does not automatically trigger trade registration
  requirements, provided no significant ancillary services are offered.

## Step 2: Trade Registration (Gewerbeanmeldung)

### 2.1 When Required
A Gewerbeanmeldung at the local Bezirksverwaltungsbehoerde (district authority)
is required when:
- You provide hotel-like ancillary services (breakfast, daily cleaning,
  reception desk, laundry service).
- You operate multiple rental units in a structured, business-like manner.
- The overall activity goes beyond simple asset management.

The relevant trade category is typically:
- **"Vermieten von Ferienwohnungen"** or more broadly
  **"Beherbergung von Gaesten"** (accommodation of guests).
- This is classified as a **freies Gewerbe** (free/unregulated trade), meaning
  no proof of qualification is needed -- only registration.

### 2.2 How to Register
1. File a Gewerbeanmeldung at the competent Bezirkshauptmannschaft (BH) or
   Magistrat (in statutory cities like Vienna, Graz, Linz, etc.).
2. Required documents:
   - Valid identification (passport or Austrian ID).
   - Proof of address / property ownership or lease.
   - If a company: company register extract (Firmenbuchauszug).
   - Criminal record extract (Strafregisterbescheinigung) -- typically
     requested automatically.
3. The registration takes effect immediately upon filing (Anzeigeverfahren).
4. Cost: Administrative fees of approximately EUR 50-100.

### 2.3 When NOT Required
If you are simply renting out a furnished apartment without ancillary services,
and the activity qualifies as Vermoegensverwaltung, no Gewerbeanmeldung is
needed. You still have all other obligations (guest registration, tourist tax,
income tax, etc.).

## Step 3: Guest Registration (Meldepflicht)

### 3.1 Legal Basis
Under the **Meldegesetz 1991** (Section 8) and corresponding state tourism laws,
every accommodation provider in Austria must register all guests within 24 hours
of arrival.

### 3.2 Data to Collect
For each guest (including children), you must record:
- Full name (family name and given names).
- Date of birth.
- Nationality.
- Passport or ID number (for foreign nationals).
- Permanent home address.
- Date of arrival and expected date of departure.
- Number of accompanying persons.

### 3.3 Gaesteverzeichnis (Guest Register)
- You must maintain a **Gaesteverzeichnis** (guest register/ledger), either
  in paper form or electronically.
- The register must be kept for 7 years and made available to authorities
  upon request.

### 3.4 Reporting to Authorities
- Guest data must be reported to the local **Meldestelle** (registration
  office) of the municipality. In many regions, this is done through the
  **feratel Guest Registration System** (see Step 4).
- Foreign nationals: Data on non-EU guests must additionally be available for
  inspection by the Fremdenpolizei (aliens police) or BFA
  (Bundesamt fuer Fremdenwesen und Asyl).

### 3.5 Deadlines
- Registration must occur within **24 hours** of the guest's arrival.
- Deregistration should occur at or shortly after departure.

## Step 4: feratel Guest Registration System

### 4.1 Overview
**feratel media technologies AG** operates the dominant electronic guest
registration system used across most Austrian municipalities and tourism
associations (Tourismusverbaende). It is widely used in Tyrol, Salzburg,
Vorarlberg, Carinthia, Styria, and Upper Austria.

### 4.2 How It Works
1. **Webclient registration:** Hosts log into the feratel webclient
   (https://webclient.feratel.at or a regional variant) to enter guest data.
2. The system automatically transmits guest data to:
   - The local Tourismusverband (tourism association).
   - The municipal Meldestelle (registration office).
   - In some regions, directly to police authorities.
3. The system also calculates and tracks **Ortstaxe** (tourist tax) owed.

### 4.3 Getting Access
- Contact your local **Tourismusverband** or **Gemeinde** (municipality)
  to obtain login credentials.
- You will need to register your property as an accommodation provider
  (Beherbergungsbetrieb) with the Tourismusverband first.
- There may be a one-time setup fee or annual membership dues to the
  Tourismusverband (typically EUR 50-300/year depending on the region
  and size of operation).

### 4.4 Alternative Systems
- **Vienna** uses its own system: the **eReg** electronic registration system
  operated by the City of Vienna (MA 62).
- Some municipalities may accept paper-based registration (Meldeschein) but
  electronic registration is increasingly mandatory.

## Step 5: Guest Card Systems (Gaestekarte)

### 5.1 Overview
Many Austrian tourism regions issue a **Gaestekarte** (guest card) to
registered overnight guests. The card provides free or discounted access to
local attractions, public transport, cable cars, swimming pools, and more.

### 5.2 Regional Guest Card Programs
Key programs include:

| Region | Card Name | Key Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Tyrol (various) | Regional guest cards (e.g., Innsbruck Card, Oetztal Card) | Free cable cars, buses, museums |
| SalzburgerLand | SalzburgerLand Card | 190+ attractions |
| Vorarlberg | Bregenzerwald Gaeste-Card | Buses, cable cars, pools |
| Carinthia | Kaernten Card | 100+ attractions |
| Styria | Schladming-Dachstein Sommercard | Cable cars, buses, pools |
| Upper Austria | Salzkammergut Erlebnis-Card | Boats, cable cars, attractions |

### 5.3 Host Obligations
- As an accommodation provider, you are typically **required** to issue
  guest cards to your registered guests.
- The cost of the guest card is usually included in the Ortstaxe or billed
  separately through the Tourismusverband.
- You must ensure every overnight guest receives a card upon check-in and
  that the card validity matches the stay duration.

## Step 6: Tourist Tax (Ortstaxe / Naechtigungstaxe)

### 6.1 Legal Basis
Each Bundesland has its own Tourismusgesetz that empowers municipalities to
levy a per-night tourist tax. The tax is typically called **Ortstaxe**,
**Naechtigungstaxe**, **Aufenthaltsabgabe**, or **Kurtaxe** depending on
the region.

### 6.2 Rates by Bundesland (approximate, subject to municipal variation)

| Bundesland | Tax Name | Typical Rate per Person/Night | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vienna** | Ortstaxe | 3.02% of accommodation price (excl. VAT) | Percentage-based, not flat rate |
| **Tyrol** | Aufenthaltsabgabe | EUR 1.00 - 3.50 | Varies by municipality/season |
| **Salzburg** | Ortstaxe | EUR 0.50 - 4.00 | Set by each municipality |
| **Vorarlberg** | Naechtigungstaxe | EUR 1.50 - 3.60 | Varies by municipality |
| **Carinthia** | Ortstaxe | EUR 0.70 - 2.50 | Varies by municipality |
| **Styria** | Naechtigungsabgabe | EUR 0.50 - 2.50 | Varies by municipality |
| **Upper Austria** | Ortstaxe | EUR 0.70 - 2.50 | Not all municipalities levy it |
| **Lower Austria** | Ortstaxe | EUR 0.50 - 2.00 | Not all municipalities levy it |
| **Burgenland** | Ortstaxe | EUR 0.50 - 1.50 | Varies by municipality |

### 6.3 Collection and Remittance
- The accommodation provider is legally required to **collect** the tax from
  guests and **remit** it to the municipality or Tourismusverband.
- Remittance periods vary: monthly, quarterly, or per-stay depending on the
  municipality.
- If using the feratel system, the tax is often calculated and tracked
  automatically based on guest registrations.

### 6.4 Exemptions
Common exemptions from tourist tax include:
- Children under a certain age (varies: under 6, under 12, or under 15
  depending on the Bundesland).
- Stays for medical treatment or rehabilitation (in some regions).
- Business travelers (in some municipalities, but this is rare in Austria).
- Stays under a minimum duration (some municipalities exempt single-night stays).

## Step 7: Vienna -- Specific Regulations

Vienna has the most comprehensive and restrictive short-term rental framework
in Austria.

### 7.1 Legal Framework
- **Wiener Tourismusfoerderungsgesetz 2024** (Vienna Tourism Promotion Act) --
  updated framework for accommodation providers.
- **Wiener Bauordnung** -- building code provisions relevant to change of use.
- The **MA 37** (building authority) oversees building permits.
- The **MA 62** (registration office) handles guest registration.

### 7.2 Key Requirements in Vienna

**Registration and Reporting:**
- All accommodation providers must register with the **City of Vienna** and
  obtain a registration number.
- Guest registration is done electronically via the **eReg system**
  (administered by MA 62).
- The Ortstaxe of 3.02% of the net accommodation price must be collected and
  remitted monthly to the City of Vienna (MA 6 -- Accounting Office).

**Building Use Change (Widmung):**
- If a residential property (Wohnnutzung) is used for short-term tourist
  accommodation, a **Nutzungsaenderung** (change of use) may be required under
  the Wiener Bauordnung.
- In Vienna, properties zoned as "Wohngebiet" (residential area) may face
  restrictions on tourist use. The building authority (MA 37) decides on a
  case-by-case basis.
- A change of use from residential to tourist accommodation requires a
  building permit (Baubewilligung).
- Community housing ("Gemeindebau" / municipal housing and subsidized housing)
  is strictly prohibited from being used for short-term rental.

**Condominium Restrictions:**
- The **Wohnungseigentumsgesetz 2002 (WEG)** allows apartment owners'
  associations (Wohnungseigentuemergemeinschaft) to restrict or prohibit
  short-term rentals if they change the "agreed use" (vereinbarter
  Verwendungszweck) of the unit.
- Austrian Supreme Court (OGH) rulings have upheld that other co-owners can
  object to Airbnb-style rentals if they constitute a change in use.

**Platform Registration:**
- Platforms operating in Vienna must report host data to the city authority.

### 7.3 Ortstaxe in Vienna
- Rate: **3.02%** of the net accommodation charge (excluding VAT and breakfast).
- Due monthly by the 15th of the following month.
- Filed via the electronic system of the City of Vienna (MA 6).
- Penalties for non-payment: fines up to EUR 2,100 per infringement.

## Step 8: Salzburg -- Specific Regulations

### 8.1 Legal Framework
- **Salzburger Tourismusgesetz 2003** (as amended).
- **Salzburger Raumordnungsgesetz 2009** -- zoning provisions.
- **Salzburger Baupolizeigesetz** -- building regulations.

### 8.2 Key Requirements

**Tourism Association Membership:**
- All accommodation providers in Salzburg must be members of the local
  **Tourismusverband** and pay annual membership fees (Tourismusbeitrag).
- The Tourismusbeitrag is calculated as a percentage of revenue or a flat
  rate depending on the category.

**Registration:**
- Guest registration via **feratel** is mandatory throughout the state of
  Salzburg.
- Register your property with the Tourismusverband to obtain feratel access.

**Ortstaxe:**
- Varies by municipality. In the city of Salzburg, the Ortstaxe is
  approximately EUR 2.00-3.65 per person per night (varies seasonally).
- Exemptions for children under 16 in many municipalities.

**Zoning (Salzburg City):**
- The city of Salzburg has introduced restrictions on new vacation rentals in
  certain historic center zones (Altstadt).
- A change of use from residential to tourist use may require approval from
  the Baubehoerde (building authority).
- The Salzburger Raumordnungsgesetz allows municipalities to designate
  "Vorbehaltsflächen fuer Wohnzwecke" (reserved residential areas) where
  tourist use is restricted or prohibited.

### 8.3 Zweitwohnsitz (Second Home) Restrictions
- Salzburg has strict **Raumordnungsrechtliche Beschraenkungen** on second
  homes (Zweitwohnsitzregelung). Properties designated as "Freizeitwohnsitz"
  (recreational residence) have caps per municipality.
- Using a Freizeitwohnsitz for commercial vacation rental may require
  additional permits.

## Step 9: Tyrol -- Specific Regulations

### 9.1 Legal Framework
- **Tiroler Tourismusgesetz 2006** (as amended).
- **Tiroler Raumordnungsgesetz 2022** -- extensive second-home and zoning
  restrictions.
- **Tiroler Bauordnung 2022** -- building code.

### 9.2 Key Requirements

**Second Home Restrictions (Freizeitwohnsitz):**
- Tyrol has the **strictest second-home regulations** in Austria.
- The Tiroler Raumordnungsgesetz limits the number of Freizeitwohnsitze
  (recreational residences) per municipality, typically to 8-12% of the
  total housing stock.
- Converting a primary residence to a vacation rental may violate the
  second-home cap and is monitored by the Grundverkehrsbehoerde (real estate
  transactions authority).
- Violations can result in orders to cease use and fines up to EUR 40,000.

**Tourism Registration:**
- All accommodation providers must register with the local Tourismusverband.
- Guest registration is done via **feratel** (mandatory).
- The Aufenthaltsabgabe (tourist tax) must be collected and remitted.

**Aufenthaltsabgabe (Tourist Tax):**
- Rates vary by municipality and season, typically EUR 1.00-3.50 per person
  per night.
- Popular tourist municipalities (e.g., Innsbruck, Kitzbuehel, St. Anton,
  Soelden) have higher rates.
- Children exemptions vary (often under 15 years).

**Zoning:**
- Many Tyrolean municipalities have designated "Vorbehaltsgemeinden" where new
  tourist accommodation is heavily restricted or requires special permits.
- A Baubewilligung (building permit) is required for any change of use from
  residential to tourist accommodation.

## Step 10: Vorarlberg -- Specific Regulations

### 10.1 Legal Framework
- **Vorarlberger Tourismusgesetz 1997** (as amended).
- **Vorarlberger Raumplanungsgesetz** -- zoning rules.
- **Vorarlberger Baugesetz** -- building code.

### 10.2 Key Requirements

**Registration:**
- All accommodation providers must register with the Tourismusverband.
- Guest registration is done via **feratel**.

**Tourist Tax:**
- The Naechtigungstaxe ranges from approximately EUR 1.50-3.60 per person
  per night, set by each municipality.
- Popular areas like Lech-Zuers, Montafon, and Bregenzerwald have higher
  rates.

**Zoning Restrictions:**
- Vorarlberg has Freizeitwohnsitz restrictions similar to Tyrol.
- The Vorarlberger Raumplanungsgesetz regulates second homes and may restrict
  conversion of residential properties to tourist use.
- Municipal Flaechenwidmungsplaene (zoning plans) determine where tourist
  accommodation is permitted.

**Bregenzerwald Gaeste-Card:**
- In the Bregenzerwald region, hosts are required to participate in the
  guest card program, with costs typically included in the Tourismusbeitrag.

## Step 11: Tax Obligations

### 11.1 Income Tax (Einkommensteuer)

**Rental income** from vacation rentals is taxable in Austria. The
classification depends on whether the activity is treated as:

- **Einkuenfte aus Vermietung und Verpachtung (Section 28 EStG):**
  Applies when the rental is treated as private asset management. Income is
  the difference between rental receipts and deductible expenses (mortgage
  interest, depreciation at 1.5% of building value per year, maintenance,
  insurance, platform fees, cleaning costs, furnishing depreciation, etc.).

- **Einkuenfte aus Gewerbebetrieb (Section 23 EStG):**
  Applies when the activity is classified as a trade (Gewerbebetrieb). This
  triggers trade registration requirements and may also result in
  Gewerbesteuer implications (though Austria does not have a separate
  municipal trade tax like Germany).

**Tax rates:** Austria uses progressive income tax rates:
| Taxable Income (EUR) | Marginal Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 12,816 | 0% |
| 12,816 - 20,818 | 20% |
| 20,818 - 34,513 | 30% |
| 34,513 - 66,612 | 40% |
| 66,612 - 99,266 | 48% |
| Over 99,266 | 50% |
| Over 1,000,000 | 55% |

**Filing:** Rental income must be declared in the annual Einkommensteuererklaerung
(income tax return), typically due by April 30 (paper) or June 30 (electronic
via FinanzOnline) of the following year.

### 11.2 Value Added Tax (Umsatzsteuer / VAT)

**VAT on short-term accommodation:**
- Short-term accommodation (up to 3 months) is subject to a **reduced VAT
  rate of 10%** in Austria (Section 10 (2) Z 4 UStG).
- This applies to the accommodation charge itself.
- Additional services (e.g., parking, laundry) may be subject to the standard
  20% VAT rate.

**Kleinunternehmerregelung (Small Business Exemption):**
- If your total annual revenue (Umsatz) does not exceed **EUR 42,000**
  (threshold increased from EUR 35,000, effective as updated in recent
  amendments to the UStG), you qualify for the Kleinunternehmerregelung.
- Under this regime, you do **not** charge VAT on your invoices and do not
  file VAT returns, but you also cannot deduct input VAT (Vorsteuer).
- You must monitor your revenue and register for VAT if the threshold is
  exceeded.
- **Important:** The EUR 42,000 threshold applies to total annual turnover
  across all economic activities, not just rental income. Verify the current
  threshold with your Steuerberater, as it has been subject to legislative
  updates.

**VAT registration:**
- If you exceed the Kleinunternehmer threshold, you must register for VAT
  at your local Finanzamt (tax office) via FinanzOnline.
- VAT returns (Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung) are due monthly or quarterly
  depending on revenue volume.
- An annual VAT return (Umsatzsteuerjahreserklaerung) is also required.

### 11.3 Tourism Levy (Tourismusbeitrag)
- Separate from the Ortstaxe collected from guests, accommodation providers
  in most Bundeslaender must pay a **Tourismusbeitrag** (tourism contribution)
  to their local Tourismusverband.
- This is typically calculated as a percentage of revenue (often 1-3%) or a
  flat amount based on bed count.
- It is a deductible business expense for income tax purposes.

## Step 12: Safety Requirements

### 12.1 Fire Safety
- All accommodation properties must comply with applicable fire safety
  regulations of the relevant Bundesland's Bauordnung.
- Minimum requirements typically include:
  - **Smoke detectors** (Rauchmelder) in all bedrooms and escape routes.
    Mandatory in most Bundeslaender (e.g., mandatory in Vienna, Tyrol,
    Salzburg, Vorarlberg).
  - **Fire extinguisher** -- recommended and often required for commercial
    accommodation.
  - **Escape routes** must be clearly marked and unobstructed.
  - **Emergency plan** (Fluchtplan) displayed in a visible location.
  - CO detectors where gas appliances are present.

### 12.2 Gas and Electrical Safety
- Gas installations must be inspected regularly (typically every 2-4 years)
  by a certified technician (Gasgeraeteueberpruefung).
- Electrical installations must comply with OVE/OENORM standards.
- In Vienna, a **Gasgeraeteueberpruefung** (gas appliance inspection) is
  mandatory every 2 years for rental properties.

### 12.3 Swimming Pools and Outdoor Areas
- If the property has a pool, hot tub, or sauna, additional safety
  regulations apply (fencing, covers, signage).
- Balconies and terraces must have appropriate railings meeting building code
  height requirements (typically minimum 100 cm).

### 12.4 General Safety
- The property must be in a structurally sound condition.
- Drinking water quality must meet Austrian standards (Trinkwasserverordnung).
- Heating systems must be regularly serviced and inspected.
- A **first aid kit** should be available in the property.
- Emergency telephone numbers must be displayed.

## Step 13: Building and Zoning Permits

### 13.1 Widmung (Zoning Designation)
Before operating a vacation rental, verify the property's zoning designation
in the municipal **Flaechenwidmungsplan** (zoning plan):
- **Bauland-Wohngebiet** (residential): Short-term tourist rental may require
  a change of use (Nutzungsaenderung / Umwidmung).
- **Bauland-Kerngebiet** (mixed use): Tourist accommodation is generally
  permitted.
- **Bauland-Sondergebiet fuer Tourismus** (special tourism zone): Specifically
  designated for tourist use.

### 13.2 Change of Use (Nutzungsaenderung)
- Converting a residential property to tourist accommodation typically requires
  a **Baubewilligung** (building permit) in most Bundeslaender.
- The application is filed with the municipal building authority
  (Baubehoerde / Magistrat).
- Requirements may include:
  - Architectural plans.
  - Fire safety concept (Brandschutzkonzept).
  - Proof of adequate parking.
  - Neighbor notification and potential objection period.
  - Environmental impact assessment (for larger projects).
- Processing times vary: typically 2-6 months.

### 13.3 Denkmalschutz (Monument Protection)
- Properties under monument protection (Denkmalschutz) require additional
  approval from the Bundesdenkmalamt (Federal Monuments Office) for any
  modifications.

## Step 14: Insurance Requirements

### 14.1 Liability Insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung)
- While not universally mandated by federal law for all rental types, **liability
  insurance** is strongly recommended and effectively essential.
- If operating as a Gewerbebetrieb (commercial operation), many Bundeslaender
  require or strongly recommend adequate liability coverage.
- Coverage should include:
  - Bodily injury to guests and third parties.
  - Property damage.
  - Key loss coverage.
  - Legal defense costs.
- Recommended minimum coverage: EUR 1-5 million.

### 14.2 Building Insurance (Gebaeudeversicherung)
- Standard building insurance covering fire, storm, water damage, and natural
  hazards.
- Check that the policy does not exclude commercial or tourist use -- many
  standard residential policies do.

### 14.3 Contents Insurance (Inhaltsversicherung)
- Covers furnishings, appliances, and equipment against damage or theft by
  guests.
- Specific "vacation rental" (Ferienwohnung) insurance products are offered
  by Austrian insurers (e.g., Allianz, Generali, Uniqa, Wiener Staedtische).

### 14.4 Business Interruption Insurance
- Optional but recommended: covers lost rental income due to insured events
  (fire, flood, etc.).

## Step 15: Platform Reporting -- DAC7

### 15.1 Overview
The EU Directive 2021/514 (**DAC7**) has been transposed into Austrian law
through amendments to the **EU-Meldepflichtgesetz (EU-MPfG)** and the
**Plattformen-Steuertransparenzgesetz (PStTG)**.

### 15.2 What It Means for Hosts
- **Digital platforms** (Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo/FeWo-direkt, etc.) are
  required to collect and report host data to tax authorities.
- Reported information includes:
  - Host identity (name, address, date of birth, tax identification number /
    Steuernummer or Sozialversicherungsnummer).
  - Property address.
  - Total consideration (revenue) paid to the host per year.
  - Number of rental transactions.
  - Fees, commissions, or taxes withheld by the platform.
- Platforms report this data to the tax authority of their country of
  registration, which then exchanges it with the Austrian Finanzamt via
  automatic information exchange.

### 15.3 Host Obligations
- Hosts must provide accurate personal and tax information to the platform.
- Platforms may block or suspend listings if hosts do not provide required
  information.
- The data reported by platforms is cross-referenced with income tax returns.
  Undeclared rental income will be flagged.

### 15.4 Timeline
- DAC7 reporting has been in effect since **January 1, 2023**. The first
  reporting period covered the calendar year 2023, with reports due by
  January 31, 2024.
- Ongoing annual reporting applies to all subsequent years.

## Step 16: Sanctions and Fines

### 16.1 Failure to Register Guests (Meldepflicht Violations)
- Fines up to **EUR 2,180** per infringement under the Meldegesetz.
- Repeated violations may result in higher penalties and potential prohibition
  of the activity.

### 16.2 Tax Evasion / Non-Declaration of Income
- Failure to declare rental income constitutes tax evasion
  (Steuerhinterziehung) or negligent tax reduction (fahrlaessige
  Abgabenverkuerzung) under the Finanzstrafgesetz (FinStrG).
- Penalties:
  - **Fahrlaessige Abgabenverkuerzung:** Fine up to the amount of the evaded
    tax.
  - **Vorsaetzliche Abgabenhinterziehung (intentional):** Fine up to **double**
    the amount of the evaded tax, plus potential imprisonment for severe cases
    (over EUR 100,000 evaded: up to 2 years; over EUR 500,000: up to 5 years).
- Voluntary self-disclosure (Selbstanzeige) before detection can avoid criminal
  penalties if all back taxes plus interest are paid.

### 16.3 Failure to Collect/Remit Tourist Tax
- Penalties vary by Bundesland. Typically fines of **EUR 500-5,000** for
  failure to collect or remit Ortstaxe/Naechtigungstaxe.
- The accommodation provider remains personally liable for uncollected tax.

### 16.4 Operating Without Required Trade Registration
- Operating a trade without Gewerbeanmeldung is an administrative offense
  under the Gewerbeordnung.
- Fines up to **EUR 3,600** per infringement.

### 16.5 Zoning and Building Code Violations
- Operating tourist accommodation without the required building permit or
  in violation of zoning regulations can result in:
  - Orders to cease the activity (Untersagungsbescheid).
  - Fines of **EUR 1,000-50,000** depending on the Bundesland.
  - In Tyrol: fines up to **EUR 40,000** for second-home/Freizeitwohnsitz
    violations.
  - Restoration orders (Wiederherstellungsauftrag) requiring the property
    to be returned to its permitted use.

### 16.6 Failure to Comply with Safety Regulations
- Penalties under the respective Bauordnung, typically administrative fines.
- In the event of an accident, the property owner faces civil liability and
  potential criminal prosecution for negligence (fahrlaessige Koerperverletzung).

### 16.7 DAC7 Non-Compliance
- Platforms that fail to report face fines under the EU-MPfG/PStTG.
- Hosts who provide false information to platforms may face penalties under
  tax fraud provisions.

## Summary Checklist

Before listing your property, ensure you have completed the following:

- [ ] Verified zoning designation (Flaechenwidmungsplan) permits tourist use.
- [ ] Obtained building permit for change of use if required.
- [ ] Checked condominium rules (WEG) for any restrictions on short-term rental.
- [ ] Filed Gewerbeanmeldung if hotel-like services are provided.
- [ ] Registered with the local Tourismusverband.
- [ ] Obtained feratel / eReg access for electronic guest registration.
- [ ] Set up system to collect and remit Ortstaxe / tourist tax.
- [ ] Registered with the Finanzamt for income tax (and VAT if above threshold).
- [ ] Obtained adequate insurance (liability, building, contents).
- [ ] Installed smoke detectors, fire extinguisher, and emergency signage.
- [ ] Completed gas and electrical safety inspections.
- [ ] Prepared guest information folder with emergency numbers and house rules.
- [ ] Provided accurate personal/tax data to booking platforms (DAC7).
- [ ] Set up bookkeeping system to track income, expenses, and tax obligations.
