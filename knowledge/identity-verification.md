# Identity Verification — European Requirements (Insights)

> Curated expert insights on guest identity verification obligations for vacation rental and tourist accommodation operators across Europe.

## Overview

Identity verification requirements vary significantly across European countries. Some require in-person document checks at check-in, others accept remote/digital verification, and the trend is moving toward stricter in-person requirements in several key markets.

---

## Italy

### Check-In Wall (New Regulation)

Italy has introduced stricter identity verification rules for tourist accommodations:

- **In-person verification is mandatory**: The guest must present identification documents **at the moment of check-in**, physically at the property.
- **No remote check-in for ID verification**: Unlike some countries that allow digital/remote identity verification before arrival, Italy now requires the host or their delegate to **verify identity face-to-face**.
- **The verification must happen at the time of entry**, not before or after.
- **Applicable to all tourist accommodation types**: Hotels, B&Bs, vacation rentals (case vacanze), and short-term lets.
- **Documents accepted**: Valid passport or national ID card (carta d'identità) for EU citizens; passport required for non-EU guests.
- **Police reporting (Alloggiati Web)**: Hosts must report guest data to the Questura (police) within 24 hours of check-in via the Alloggiati Web portal.

### Practical Implications for Hosts

- **Self-check-in with lockboxes alone is NOT compliant** — someone must be present to verify identity.
- Hosts can delegate to a property manager or co-host, but identity verification must still be done in person.
- Penalties for non-compliance can include fines and potential suspension of tourist rental activity.

### Key Cities

- **Rome**: High enforcement, regular inspections.
- **Milan**: Active enforcement, especially in central areas.
- **Florence**: Strict compliance expected, tourist police active.
- **Naples, Venice, Lucca, Bologna**: Standard national rules apply.

## Spain

### General Framework

- Guest identity must be verified and reported to police via **SES.HOSPEDAJES** (formerly Policía Nacional / Guardia Civil parts).
- **Remote identity verification is accepted** in most regions — guests can submit ID digitally before arrival.
- Hosts must register all guests over 14 years old.
- Data must be reported within **24 hours of check-in**.
- Required data: full name, nationality, ID/passport number, date of birth, date of entry.

### By Region (Key Differences)

- **All regions**: Must comply with SES.HOSPEDAJES national reporting.
- **Cataluña**: Additional Mossos d'Esquadra reporting may apply.
- **Baleares**: Standard national framework.
- **Canarias**: Standard national framework.

---

## Portugal

- Guest registration is mandatory via **SEF (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras)** system, now managed by AIMA.
- Hosts must register guests within **3 working days**.
- **Digital verification accepted** — can be done remotely.
- Applicable to all Alojamento Local (AL) properties.

---

## France

- **Fiche individuelle de police**: Required for foreign guests at hotels and tourist residences.
- Guests must fill out a police form with personal details.
- Less strictly enforced for vacation rentals (meublés de tourisme) compared to hotels.

## Common Patterns Across Europe

| Requirement | Italy | Spain | Portugal | France |
|-------------|-------|-------|----------|--------|
| In-person ID check | **Required** | Not required | Not required | Hotels only |
| Digital/remote verification | **Not allowed** | Allowed | Allowed | N/A for rentals |
| Police reporting | Within 24h | Within 24h | Within 3 days | At check-in |
| Reporting system | Alloggiati Web | SES.HOSPEDAJES | SEF/AIMA | Fiche de police |
| Minimum age to register | All guests | 14+ | All guests | Foreign guests |

## Common Questions

- "Do I need to verify ID in person?" → Depends on the country. Italy: yes. Spain/Portugal: digital accepted.
- "Can I use self-check-in?" → In Italy: only if someone verifies ID in person separately. In Spain/Portugal: yes, with remote ID verification.
- "What documents are accepted?" → Passport (always), national ID card (EU citizens), residence permit (varies).
- "What happens if I don't register guests?" → Fines vary by country. In Italy and Spain, can be significant (€100-€600+ per unregistered guest).
